## D. Module 3 — Monolithic Coupling (land-surface AND embedded-feature exchanges)

### D.1 Role and scope
Module 3 is the **assembler**. It owns no new state of its own; it composes the residuals and Jacobians produced by Module 1 (subsurface Richards), Module 2 (overland), and the embedded-feature exchange terms authored by Module 4 into **one** global residual `F(U)=0` and its Jacobian `J=∂F/∂U` over a **single blocked/mixed function space**, solved by **one** Newton (PETSc SNES) iteration per timestep. Two families of *smooth, potential-driven* exchange live inside that residual: (i) the **land-surface exchange** between ponded overland water and the top subsurface cells, and (ii) the **feature↔soil per-face exchange** (terms physically owned by Module 4 but injected into this same monolithic residual). Module 3 specifies *how both families are composed*, the global mass-balance accounting across **both** interface families, and the coupled preconditioner. Solver internals (SNES, SNES-VI, line search, adaptive `dt`) are referenced to Section H (cross-cutting-numerics), not re-specified.
