# Plan B — Infiltration & Recharge for Perennial Crops

**Date:** 2026-06-08 · **Status:** 🟢 Developed (3 research agents synthesized). Benefit quantification deferred to model completion.
**Parent:** [`vertical-assessment.md`](vertical-assessment.md) · [`fundraising-plan.md`](fundraising-plan.md)

## Framing — benefit hierarchy (Arik, 2026-06-08)

1. **PRIMARY — increased infiltration (more water to the root zone) + recharge (more groundwater for
   irrigation).** Capture rainfall/applied water into the root zone and the local aquifer the grower
   draws on — the value is *water availability* for high-value crops.
2. **SECONDARY — more efficient drainage of temporarily waterlogged crops** (root-zone protection from
   transient saturation).
3. **TERTIARY — sustainable agriculture** (passive, natural materials, water stewardship).

**Crops:** **perennial only at first — blueberry, almond, wine grape** (high per-acre value; acute
drainage/water sensitivity; budgeted at planting). Row crops deferred.

**GTM model:** PIDS = a software/design *tool* used by **certified ag-drainage / vineyard-orchard
site-prep installers**; grower-decided, generally non-permitted on-farm work; design-partner/pilot
motion to build field evidence.

## 0. The central tension (state this honestly up front)

PIDS's **strongest soil fit** (clay/poorly-drained) and its **strongest water-scarcity demand**
(CA almond/grape under SGMA) sit largely on **different ground** — the San Joaquin Valley is mostly
*permeable* sandy-loam where cheap Flood-MAR basins already work; clay is the exception there. The
"passive capture into clay + recharge the irrigated aquifer" sweet spot is a **narrower intersection**
than either headline implies. **→ Build the SAM on *clay-soil acreage being newly established/
replanted in water-stressed or disease-prone regions*, not on total crop acreage.**

## 1. Market & TAM

| Crop | Size / structure | Water situation → which PIDS benefit leads |
|---|---|---|
| **Blueberry** | US ~$1.15B, ~105K ac; WA/OR/GA ≈ ⅔ of value; **NC ~8–9K ac (~$105M), concentrated in Bladen/Pender** (home turf). | A **DRAINAGE** story, not scarcity: roots suffocate if waterlogged days → Phytophthora; clay is hostile (growers bed/ditch or *avoid* clay). → PIDS's **SECONDARY** (drainage) benefit leads here. |
| **Almond** | CA ≈100% of US; ~1.39M bearing ac; **$5.66B (~$4K/ac)**; contracting (new plantings **−68% since 2022**); >80% in SJV. | **Strongest SCARCITY signal** (SGMA pumping caps below orchard need) → PIDS's **PRIMARY** (recharge-for-irrigation) benefit — *but* soil overlap is weak (permeable ground; clay/hardpan pockets only). |
| **Wine grape** | CA contracting hard (590K→**540K ac, −7%/yr**; ~40K ac removed; crush 25-yr low; ~50% no contract). | **Best conceptual fit** (clay hillside vineyards → primary recharge *and* secondary drainage coexist) — but a **shrinking, capital-starved** market. |

SAM = *clay/poorly-drained acreage being established or replanted* in these crops → realistically **low
tens of thousands of acres**, addressed at a one-time per-acre design/install fee comparable to
drainage/site-prep spend ($1–4K/ac physical works; our slice is the design fee). A niche-within-a-niche
that we win on depth, not breadth. *(Figures unverified; no PIDS performance asserted.)*

## 2. Where we fit / positioning

**The whitespace:** existing tools each do only ONE of {drain excess} OR {recharge aquifer} OR
{irrigate efficiently}, and the recharge tools (Flood-MAR/basins) need *permeable* soil. PIDS's
hypothesized position is the **passive, permanent, dual-direction primitive on poorly-drained CLAY** —
move water into the clay root zone *and* down to recharge the very aquifer the grower pumps, *and*
relieve transient waterlogging — installed once at site-prep through the existing certified-installer
channel, no pumps/power.

**Fit by crop:** wine grape (clay regions) = best *conceptual* fit but contracting market; almond =
strongest *demand* / weakest *soil overlap* (clay/hardpan pockets); blueberry = clearest *drainage*
pain and the **home-market beachhead** (lead with drainage, not recharge).

## 3. Competition

- **Blueberry:** raised beds (the default clay fix), subsoiling/deep-ripping, conventional tile
  ($1–4K/ac), **site selection — growers simply avoid clay (the "free competitor" to displace)**,
  drip + soil-moisture sensing.
- **Almond (primary = supply/recharge):** **Flood-MAR / on-farm recharge basins** (the direct
  competitor — cheap on suitable soil; incentives $30–100/AF; ReNeM ~$570/AF), recharge banking
  credits, microirrigation ($1.8–3.5K/ac), soil-moisture/ET sensing (CropX, Tule, Hortau),
  surface-water purchase / fallowing. **EQIP covers 50–75% of qualifying efficiency installs** — a
  subsidy PIDS doesn't yet plug into.
- **Wine grape:** vineyard tile (gravel-enveloped pipe), **mole drains (cheapest)**, deep ripping at
  establishment, on-farm recharge/banking, deficit-drip + sensing.

## 4. Regulatory constraints & pressures

**Demand-driving (the tailwind):** **CA SGMA is the single strongest driver.** GSAs are imposing hard
pumping allocations (~0.66–1.43 AF/ac, below mature-orchard need); **recharge earns pumping credits**
(landowners typically recover 50–90% of recharged volume as future-pumping credit; some districts pay
per AF). On-farm recharge converts an SGMA *liability* (allocation cap) into *recoverable supply* — the
GTM thesis for almond/grape. Outside CA there's no SGMA-style credit (GA Floridan-aquifer withdrawal
suspension is a scarcity signal without a credit mechanism; OR/WA prior-appropriation).

**Constraints:**
- **UIC Class V is largely *avoidable by design*** — surface infiltration *wider-than-deep* is generally
  not a "well"; **the vertical tunnels (deeper-than-wide) are the element at risk** of being classed a
  Class V recharge/drainage well. Off-ramp is light (authorize-by-rule + RWQCB concurrence in CA).
- **Water rights — the defensible selling point:** recharging **your own field for your own later use**
  sits inside the **overlying groundwater right** (CA) and **avoids the appropriative-permit/banking
  morass** that selling/transferring recharged water triggers. Caveats: source water should be the
  grower's *own* runoff/rainfall; GSA allocation still governs pumping; **prior-appropriation states
  (OR/WA)** can require a right just to *store for later recovery*.
- **Nitrate / CV-SALTS** limits the *quality* of water infiltrated in the Central Valley (USDW
  protection) — source-water quality belongs in the installer design protocol.
- **Swampbuster — perennials are categorically *advantaged*** (a converted wetland is exempt if planted
  to a non-annual-tilled crop like an orchard/vineyard) → **reinforces the perennial-only strategy**;
  still require an **AD-1026 / NRCS wetland determination** before any drainage-oriented install.
  Post-*Sackett* WOTUS trend is favorable.

**Funding (gated, with off-ramps):** EQIP/CSP pay only against **approved practice standards** → a
novel system isn't automatically eligible. Off-ramps: **(i)** CA NRCS has built **interim recharge
practice standards** under EQIP — a PIDS install may map onto one (best near-term path; confirm codes);
**(ii) CIG (Conservation Innovation Grants)** is the *designed* vehicle for novelty → **CIG pilot →
field data → new/amended practice standard → EQIP/CSP at scale.** Also EQIP-WaterSMART, Irrigation
Water Management (449), RCPP (bundle via a district/GSA).

## 5. Market strategy (GTM)

**Beachhead: NC/Southeast blueberry FIRST** (proof + cashflow), **leading with the fast-to-prove
DRAINAGE benefit** (single-season, visible root-rot/waterlogging pain) — *not* the multi-season
recharge benefit. **Run CA almond as a parallel, lower-intensity "lighthouse" track** for the
SGMA-recharge narrative (highly fundable) + future expansion. **De-prioritize wine grape** (contracting,
capex-starved). **Sequence: blueberry NC → MI/PNW → almond CA → opportunistic premium-replant grape.**

*Why blueberry first:* acute single-season pain; **founder proximity** (NC #7 producer; ~6K of ~10K ac
in Bladen County alone — a drive-to, dense, relationship market with NC State extension already
publishing the problem); drainage is *already a paid line item* (bedding/ditching); MI/PNW give the
expansion runway.

**Channel — the decision is *intermediated*, so arm the channel, don't disintermediate it:**
- **Ag-drainage / site-prep contractors = the certified-installer network, and it's highly credible:**
  firms like Ag Drainage Inc. ("Resin-to-Outlet" design+install **warranty**), Ellingson, Ag Tech
  Drainage (early LIDAR adopter), Alleghany (serves vineyards/orchards) **already sell engineered
  designs and carry install liability** → PIDS slots in as the **design/survey upgrade they license**,
  not a behavior change. Capture the install at the **planting/replant moment** via orchard/vineyard
  site-prep firms.
- **PCAs / CCAs / crop advisers = the recommendation gatekeepers** (esp. CA); **university extension
  (NC State, UC ANR) = the trust-manufacturing layer.** Frame PIDS where **production + conservation
  overlap** (a yield/risk-protection practice that also recharges) — the zone where advisers are most
  influential.

**Pilot motion — the proven playbook (analog: Sustainable Conservation / Almond Board on-farm
recharge + the SAGBI suitability tool):** lighthouse design partners → co-instrumented side-by-side
plots → university/extension validation → demonstration sites as the sales asset, ideally grant-
co-funded (FFAR/CIG pattern). **★ Key product insight: PIDS's drone+model *is* the SAGBI analog — sell
the SITE-SUITABILITY SCREEN as a cheap standalone first product** ("we'll tell you if/where PIDS works
on your ground before you commit capital"). It generates early revenue, de-risks the buyer, and means
we **never sell the banking benefit blind.** Set investor expectations to *reference accounts + multi-
season cohorts*, not a hockey stick (CropX took ~10 yr to 20K users via dealers; precision-ag adoption
has been over-forecast for 20 yr).

## 6. Other salient concerns

1. **Soil-vs-scarcity mismatch (core risk)** — the defensible SAM is the *clay × stressed-region*
   intersection, smaller than the "$5.66B almond / SGMA recharge" headlines. Underwrite on clay-soil
   establish/replant acreage.
2. **Multi-season proof burden (highest GTM risk)** → beachhead on blueberry *drainage* (single-season
   proof) + the suitability-screen-first product + co-instrumented plots with grant co-funding.
3. **Strong, subsidized incumbents** — Flood-MAR is cheap on suitable soil + earns recharge credits;
   tile/mole drains are mature and cheap (same contractors). PIDS must articulate the *clay-specific*
   advantage and plug into EQIP cost-share + SGMA credits competitors already monetize.
4. **Counter-cyclical target markets** — grape −7%/yr, almond plantings −68%; new establishment (when a
   site-prep tool is bought) is *shrinking* in 2 of 3 crops → weight replant timing + the NC blueberry
   home market; treat CA recharge as a higher-WTP but soil-constrained *expansion* bet.
5. **Grower capex aversion** → attach to capex-already-committed moments (planting/replant); OPEX-style
   pricing; cost-share.
6. **Soil-moisture-banking *reach* uncertainty** → the suitability screen (commit only where the
   Module-4 model says it works); never market banking blind. *(Direct dependency on Module-4.)*
7. **Channel behavior-change / over-forecast adoption** → founding certified installers + warranty-
   backed bundle; reference-led growth.
8. **UIC vertical-tunnel geometry** + **AD-1026 wetland check** as installer-protocol gates.

## 7. ALTERNATIVE target — district/utility-scale Managed Aquifer Recharge (water-limited regions)

Instead of the on-farm, grower-decided perennial entry, target **institutional MAR at district/utility
scale** in critically water-limited regions. **Buyer shifts** from the individual grower to **GSAs,
irrigation/water districts, water agencies, and municipalities** (growers *host* the recharge).

- **Where:** **CA Central Valley = the premier MAR market** (SGMA mandate to reach sustainability by
  2040–42; SJV chronic deficit ~2M AF/yr; recharge is the regulator-blessed survival behavior; the
  Flood-MAR program + GSA pumping-credit economics already exist; billions in water-supply value).
  **Arizona** = a *mature* recharge regime worth studying (Active Management Areas; CAP/SRP recharge
  facilities; ADWR Underground Storage Facility + Water Storage permits; **Long-Term Storage Credits**).
  **Utah** = emerging (rising scarcity, less infrastructure).
- **PIDS fit:** where conventional recharge basins **underperform** — low-permeability / restrictive-
  layer sites, or **land-constrained** sites where open basins are too land-hungry — engineered vertical
  pathways could raise recharge per acre/footprint. **Honest tension (already flagged):** much of the
  SJV is *permeable* sandy-loam where cheap Flood-MAR basins already work, and AZ already runs engineered
  recharge facilities → **PIDS's edge is confined to clay/restrictive or space-limited sites.**
- **Competition:** Flood-MAR & recharge basins (cheap on suitable soil), dedicated recharge facilities,
  **ASR / injection wells** (Class V), dry/vadose-zone wells, in-channel & in-lieu recharge — most
  state/federally subsidized.
- **Regulatory:** *bigger but with tailwinds* — appropriative permits for diverted **source water** (CA
  has streamlined 5-yr stormwater-capture permits + AB 2026); **UIC Class V** for any well-geometry
  recharge (authorize-by-rule + RWQCB concurrence in CA; ADWR permitting in AZ); strong funding (CA water
  bonds, IIJA water infrastructure, Bureau of Reclamation **WaterSMART**, district capital).
- **Buyer / GTM reality:** an **institutional sale** — GSAs/districts are **slow, RFP- and board-driven,
  politically cautious, and demand multi-season monitored data**; a solo NC founder has no CA water-
  politics network. Mitigate via **partnership** (hydrogeology/engineering firms; sub to an established
  recharge contractor) and a **CIG/grant-funded instrumented pilot**. Upside: one district contract is
  large, mission-critical, and repeatable, and the buyer is sophisticated/values modeling.
- **Verdict:** the **biggest TAM + clearest scarcity pull + strongest investor narrative** — but the
  **slowest, most institutional sale, heaviest proof + permitting burden, a real soil-overlap tension
  (basins already work on permeable ground), and farthest from the founder's network/runway.** Best
  framed as the **venture-scale expansion prize / TAM narrative**, pursued through partnerships + a
  grant-funded pilot — *not* the pre-seed cash beachhead (which remains NC blueberry drainage). The
  **suitability-screen product** is the natural low-risk entry even here: model which recharge sites
  basins *can't* serve well, and target only those.

## 8. Adversarial review (2026-06-08) — verdict: would NOT fund as written

A hostile-VC + ag-water-economist + hydrogeologist review. *"The plan's intellectual honesty is its best
feature and its death sentence — it has already documented every reason a skeptic would pass."*

**Kill-shots (each cheaply testable):**
1. **The banking-reach physics is plausibly uneconomic in clay — and you can falsify it THIS WEEK with
   the engine you already have.** Passive sorptive uptake into low-K clay gives a short wetted radius
   (drip comparator ≈ ~0.75 m; clay emitters spaced 24–48"). If PIDS channels must sit ~1–2 m apart to
   wet a field, that's **trenching the entire field** — absurd vs. the water delivered. *Test (one
   afternoon, $0): run a 1-D/2-D Richards sorptive-imbibition sim (Modules 1–3 are done; Vogel-2001
   clay params you already use) for a passive sand channel → report steady wetted half-width + time-to-
   wet. Make that number a GATE, not a deferred Module-4 footnote.* The plan deferring this is deferring
   its own falsification.
2. **The defensible SAM is a rounding error.** clay × stressed-region × *establishing-now*, two of three
   shrinking: NC blueberry replant ≈ 500–750 ac/yr (clay subset less) → a **design-fee TAM of
   ~$150–225K/yr** (a lifestyle consultancy, not a venture); almond non-bearing acreage **104,900 and
   falling**; grape establishment **negative** (~40K ac/yr razed). *Test: overlay SSURGO clay polygons ×
   crop × establishment turnover (USDA data, pullable today).*
3. **The recharge prop may be permit-gated and contamination-exposed.** CA SWRCB: **captured
   surface-water/runoff stored underground is legally surface water in storage → generally needs an
   appropriative permit** (the overlying-right "off-ramp" the plan leans on may NOT cover the very
   rainfall/runoff-capture behavior it sells); and the **vertical tunnels are Class V by geometric
   definition** (deeper-than-wide). *Test: one scoped CA water-rights + UIC memo (bundle with the no-PE
   opinion).*

**Other top vulnerabilities:** incumbents are cheaper + subsidized (Flood-MAR ~$100/AF on permeable
ground recharged 7.6M AF in 2023; **mole drains/deep-ripping = century-old prior art on clay**);
**blueberry is a *negative-selection* market** (growers *avoid* clay + use raised beds — you'd be asking
them to choose a clay site they'd reject, on an unproven promise); SGMA economics often favor **fallowing
the worst block** over capex on the tightest soil; **5–10 yr channel/proof slog** (CropX took ~10 yr;
precision-ag adoption over-forecast for 20 straight years). It also notes the company's *own*
`vertical-assessment.md` ranked **ag-MAR dead last (34/49)** — "you are pitching the vertical your own
scorecard rejected."

**What must be true (≈5 low-probability conditions in series):** Module-4 returns a wetted radius large
enough for economic spacing (must *contradict* textbook short-reach-in-clay) · a clay × establishing-now ×
stressed sub-population large enough to be a venture · growers deliberately choose clay sites · the
recharge use stays out of appropriative-permit + Class V regimes · a CIG-funded multi-season pilot yields
an NRCS practice standard before runway ends.

**Hardening recommendations:** (a) **falsify the physics first, this week** (the imbibition sim — make
wetted-radius a gate); (b) **lead with drainage only, drop "recharge into clay" from the primary thesis**
until the model supports it — then beat mole drains on a concrete $/performance claim; (c) **redo the SAM
bottom-up** (likely confirms ag-MAR is the worst beachhead and the residential/grounds clay wedge should
lead — the two docs are in tension and the appendix is more honest); (d) **price the suitability screen as
the actual product**; (e) **get the CA water-rights + UIC memo before any almond/grape pitch**; (f) **kill
§7 district-MAR as anything but a TAM slide**; (g) reset to a **grant-funded R&D framing** if the physics
gate passes only marginally.
