# Closing the Commercialization-Credibility Gap — Co-founder vs. Advisor vs. Alternatives

**Date:** 2026-06-08 · **Status:** Decision analysis (open)
**Parent:** [`fundraising-plan.md`](fundraising-plan.md) §3, §8

## Why this matters

The research surfaced **one** team red flag that recurs across every pre-seed source: *a solo,
science-heavy / research-only founder with no commercialization credibility.* It is the single most
common deeptech pre-seed reject reason, and investors screen for it explicitly. Closing it is listed
as a **trigger milestone** (§3) — i.e., it can be the thing that *opens* the active raise, not just a
nice-to-have. This is also the least-reversible decision in the whole plan, so it's worth getting
right before we scaffold materials around a team story.

## What investors are *actually* screening for

Not a title. They're testing three things:
1. **Can this team get the science to market?** Someone must own customers, pricing, channel,
   procurement, deployment — not as a slide, as a capability.
2. **Is the founder coachable / self-aware?** Recognizing the gap and addressing it is itself a
   positive signal; pretending it doesn't exist is the negative one.
3. **Commitment & durability of the fix.** A full-time committed person reads very differently from a
   name on an advisor slide.

The implication: the *form* of the fix matters less than whether it convincingly answers #1–#3. That
opens up more options than the binary "co-founder vs. advisor."

## The option space (four, not two)

### Option A — Commercialization **co-founder** (full-time, founding equity)
- **Pros:** fully retires the red flag; investors strongly prefer ≥2 founders; real execution
  coverage (you stay on the science/tech); shared load and resilience; strongest possible signal.
- **Cons:** large, hard-to-reverse equity grant (commonly ~20–40% of founder equity); founder-fit
  risk is the #1 cause of startup death; finding the *right* person is slow and could delay the raise;
  must be structured with **vesting + 1-yr cliff** to be reversible-ish.
- **Best when:** you find someone genuinely additive and trustworthy *and* the commercial work is a
  full-time job from day one (it is, for B2G/B2B infrastructure sales).

### Option B — Commercialization **advisor(s)** (part-time, advisory shares)
- **Pros:** cheap (~0.25–1.0% each, vesting over 1–2 yrs); fast; flexible; can add credible
  names/logos for the deck; low-risk way to *test* a relationship.
- **Cons:** **does not fully retire the red flag** — investors know advisors aren't operators;
  limited bandwidth; no real execution coverage; "advisor" inflation is a known weak signal if
  overused.
- **Best when:** you need credibility + specific doors opened, and you (or a future hire) will do the
  actual commercial work.

### Option C — **Fractional** commercial exec (fractional CCO/COO/BD, contract)
- **Pros:** real operator doing real work, part-time; no founder-equity hit (cash or small equity);
  faster than a co-founder search; can convert to co-founder/full-time later.
- **Cons:** costs cash you may not have pre-raise; less "skin in the game" signal than a co-founder;
  may not satisfy investors looking for a committed founding team.
- **Best when:** you want execution coverage now but aren't ready to commit founder equity, and can
  fund a modest retainer (a grant like NC IDEA could underwrite this).

### Option D — **Budget the raise to hire** a commercial leader post-close
- **Pros:** no pre-raise equity/cash commitment; lets the raise itself fund the hire; keeps optionality.
- **Cons:** **doesn't answer the red flag at pitch time** — you're asking investors to fund a gap on
  promise; weakest of the four for the actual raise.
- **Best when:** combined with A/B/C — e.g., an advisor now + "use of funds includes a Head of
  Commercial" in the deck.

## The PIDS-specific commercial profile needed

This is *not* generic SaaS GTM. The commercial person must be credible in some mix of:
- **Municipal / public-sector procurement** (suburban drainage, roads/sidewalks) — long cycles, RFPs,
  CWSRF/stormwater-grant-funded buyers.
- **Channel / contractor / installer ecosystems** (the system gets *installed* — construction &
  deployment ops matter).
- **Sector buyers:** sports-field/turf management; ag (growers, irrigation/drainage distributors).
- **Infrastructure/hardware commercialization** generally — pilots → references → scaled deployment.

A person with water/infrastructure/construction or public-sector-sales pedigree is worth far more
here than a generic startup "growth" profile. This narrows the search but raises the quality bar.

## The pivotal unknown: *Arik's own* commercial capacity

The right answer hinges on a question only Arik can answer: **how much of the commercial role can /
does he want to own himself?**
- If Arik has meaningful commercial/customer/BD instinct and *wants* that seat → the gap may be
  smaller than the red flag implies; an **advisor or fractional** (Option B/C) plus Arik fronting
  commercial conversations may suffice, with a post-raise hire (D) for scale.
- If Arik is, and wants to remain, the deep-tech/science engine → the gap is real and structural; a
  **co-founder (A)** is the highest-value fix, with an advisor (B) as the interim while the search runs.

## Recommended default (pending Arik's self-assessment)

A **staged path that keeps optionality and de-risks fit**:
1. **Now:** recruit 1–2 *credible, specific* commercialization **advisors** (Option B) — water/
   infrastructure/public-sector operators — for credibility + door-opening, structured as a possible
   **"co-founder audition."**
2. **In parallel:** run a deliberate **co-founder search** (Option A) *only* if Arik wants to stay on
   the science. Convert the best-fit advisor → co-founder if the working relationship proves out (this
   is the lowest-risk way to acquire a co-founder — you've already test-driven the fit).
3. **In the deck:** show the advisor(s) + a clear "use of funds includes a senior commercial hire"
   (Option D) so the gap is visibly, credibly addressed even before a co-founder is locked.
4. **Fractional (C)** as the bridge if execution can't wait and a grant can fund the retainer.

This satisfies the three things investors screen for (capability, self-awareness, commitment) without
forcing a premature, irreversible founder-equity grant before fit is proven.
