# Plan A — Ponding Alleviation (Residential · Parks · Sports Fields)

**Date:** 2026-06-08 · **Status:** 🟢 Developed (3 research agents synthesized). Benefit quantification deferred to model completion.
**Parent:** [`vertical-assessment.md`](vertical-assessment.md) · [`fundraising-plan.md`](fundraising-plan.md)

## Framing — benefit hierarchy (Arik, 2026-06-08)

1. **PRIMARY — more efficient alleviation of surface ponding** (superior, cheaper drainage of standing
   water / soggy ground). This is the pain we sell to.
2. **SECONDARY — increased infiltration / recharge** (keeps water on-site, recharges groundwater &
   soil) — positioned as a *pro-sustainability positive*, not the lead.
3. **TERTIARY — natural materials** (sand/gravel vs. plastic/concrete) — a sustainability differentiator.

**Segments:** residential yards · public parks & municipal grounds · sports/athletic fields (+ schools,
HOA, institutional grounds as adjacencies).

**GTM model (from vertical assessment):** PIDS = a software/design *tool* (drone + hydrologic model →
optimal drainage-network design) used by **certified landscape/drainage installers** who install and
carry liability; clay-soil focus; **local-first in the NC Piedmont.** Lead with cost + performance +
sustainability; not drone/AI gimmickry; not water-quality claims.

> the forward-model is fully built. This plan develops the *market* (TAM, competition, regulatory,
> GTM); quantified PIDS benefits come later.

## 1. Market & TAM

**Top-of-funnel = the clay / poor-drainage problem.** ~$15B/yr in expansive-soil damage (older ASCE
figure, directional); USDA: ~half of US homes are built on expansive soils. The worst clay belt
(Southeast "red/yellow" soils, Gulf Coastal Plain, TX Blackland Prairie) **overlaps the NC-based
beachhead** — structurally favorable. No clean published "yard-drainage TAM" exists; size bottom-up
(clay-soil homes × conversion × job value × the design-fee layer we capture).

| Segment | Market anchor | Our serviceable layer |
|---|---|---|
| **Residential** | US landscaping services **$188B (2025)**; ~556–693K landscaping firms (the installer base); typical full drainage job **~$4,600** ($800–$15K). | Design/spec fee on clay-soil drainage jobs in target geographies — asset-light, not crew-constrained. Plausibly a multi-$B annual *job* flow; SOM = our fee slice. |
| **Parks / municipal** (+ schools/HOA) | US stormwater need **~$67B/20yr**, ~**$8B/yr funding gap** (~7,500 MS4s); parks chronically underfunded (~0.6–2% of budgets). | Infiltration/ponding share of GSI-eligible park & grounds projects; design-fee layer. Budget- & procurement-gated. |
| **Sports fields** | US sports-field *construction* **$4.2B (2025), only 675 firms, −7.5% CAGR** (cyclically depressed). | Budget-tier natural-grass fields on clay (can't afford $200–600K sand reconstruction). Smallest, most contested. |

## 2. Where we fit / positioning

**PIDS = passive, on-site, native-soil-preserving drainage for clay/poorly-drained ground** — where
the conventional toolkit either needs a gravity outlet/sewer it doesn't have (French drains, catch
basins), *saturates* (dry wells), needs power (sumps, SubAir), or needs expensive full-soil
replacement (sand-based fields). Sold as a drone-survey + model → design spec to the existing
installer base. **Frame product makers (ADS/NDS, Hydraway) as component-supply partners, not rivals**
(PIDS can spec their pipe/chambers inside an optimized network).

**Whitespace ranked:** ① residential clay yards (SE/Gulf) — lead; ② parks/municipal + school/HOA —
best *narrative*/funding fit; ③ sports fields — selective credibility flagships.

## 3. Competition

**Residential (incumbent map):** French drain (the default, $10–100/lf, ~$45 avg) · regrading
($1,025–$3,300) · catch basins/area drains ($1–4K/basin — *needs an outlet*) · **dry well** ($500–
$4,350 — closest conceptual cousin, *but clogs/saturates in clay → our key failure-mode warning*) ·
sump system (active — *the antithesis of passive*) · channel drains ($30–150/lf). Supply chain
consolidating: **ADS acquired NDS (~$1B)** → ADS/NDS/Infiltrator is the dominant platform (potential
ally).
**Parks/municipal:** same toolkit at scale via civil/landscape-architecture firms, badged as
"green stormwater infrastructure" (bioretention/infiltration basins).
**Sports fields (most sophisticated incumbents):** sand-based rootzone ($400–600K/field, the gold
standard) · sand-cap ($200–300K) · **sand-slit** (closest conceptual relative — engineered
preferential paths) · herringbone tile · **Hydraway** strip drains · **SubAir** vacuum (active,
~$625K). PIDS won't beat a USGA sand profile on a championship venue — wedge is the budget tier.

## 4. Regulatory constraints & pressures

**Constraints (what limits us):**
- **Land-disturbance/grading permits:** NC SPCA triggers at **1 acre**; local programs often stricter
  (**12,000 sq ft**, e.g. Raleigh). **Most residential yard jobs fall below both** → no permit. Watch
  lower local thresholds + the "common plan of development" aggregation rule.
- **EPA UIC Class V — the axis unique to *infiltrating* water (PIDS's defining trait).** Federally
  **authorize-by-rule** (no individual permit) if you file inventory and don't endanger a USDW. The
  **geometry test is gating:** trench-like *lateral channels* are likely **not** wells; **vertical
  tunnels (depth > width) likely read as Class V "stormwater drainage wells,"** and perforated
  distribution piping independently triggers the "subsurface fluid distribution system" prong. **NC**
  re-allowed stormwater injection in 2012 (15A NCAC 02C .0227) and — critically — *"stormwater
  infiltration systems subject to state/local stormwater regulations do not need a separate UIC
  permit"* (rooftop-runoff or designed-to-stormwater-regs), with "no untreated stormwater directly
  into an aquifer" + 02L groundwater-quality conditions.
- **Novel-method gate — the residential/public split:** **private residential sub-threshold work has
  NO approved-BMP/PE gate.** **Public** parks/schools require approved-product/design-manual fit and
  PE sign-off → the unlock is **NC DEQ's NEST program** (blanket SCM approval; Silva Cell precedent) +
  MDC conformance (72-hr dewater, bypass, pretreatment). *(Ties to the open no-PE legal question.)*
- **Liability:** NC follows the **reasonable-use rule** (*Pendergrast v. Aiken*, 1977). Because PIDS
  *infiltrates on-site* rather than diverting water onto a neighbor, it's comparatively **protective**;
  residual risk = mounding/lateral migration to an adjacent lot (a siting/design concern).

**Demand-driving pressures (tailwinds):** federal GSI / "keep water on site" policy + **~$46B IIJA+IRA
stormwater funding (2022–26)** incl. green-BMP grants; **MS4 (NPDES Phase II)** obligations on
municipal grounds; **stormwater utility fees + credits** — tax-exempt schools/govt and HOA common
areas *do pay*, and on-site retention earns credits (~42–50%), an ROI lever **but only for recognized
SCMs** (→ NEST again; weak for flat-fee single-family); sustainability/"natural materials" procurement
tilt (rewards the tertiary benefit).

**Net by segment:** Residential = **easiest** (sell now; disciplines = stay sub-threshold, treat
vertical tunnels/piping as the UIC-sensitive elements, file inventory where they read as wells,
on-site-infiltration helps on liability). Private sports = **medium**. Public = **hardest gate,
strongest pull** (NEST converts the MS4/credit/grant tailwind into addressable demand).

## 5. Market strategy (GTM)

**Sequencing: Residential (NC Piedmont clay) → private sports fields → public/municipal last.**
Residential wins first on every pre-seed axis (single accessible buyer, acute dense pain, existing
spend & channel, fast repeatable proof). Public is the strategic prize but a *credentialing project*
(NEST + MDC) — plant relationships early, monetize later. Sports tracks its owner (private→residential-
like; public→public-like).

**Network build — copy the right archetype:**
- **COPY: manufacturer-branded authorized-contractor model** (Belgard/Unilock) — referral-based
  recruitment, mandatory certification + training, a **workmanship/design warranty PIDS backs**, and
  **PIDS-funneled leads** ("leads without spending a dime" — the key recruiting lure). Plus an
  **Aurora-Solar-style bid-winning design tool** (land-and-expand: the drone-survey + model package is
  a sales asset that raises the installer's win-rate, not a back-office cost).
- **AVOID: the commissioned solar-dealer/lead-broker model** — it imported the Titan-Solar trust
  collapse (overselling, no accountability for performance, 100+ failed installers). Tie pay to
  delivered quality; the installer who does the work carries the warranty; no markup-on-markup layer.
- **EnergySage-style marketplace** = a *later-stage* lead-routing layer once metro density exists.

**Cold-start fix:** generate demand yourself and **hand leads free/cheap to 1–3 anchor Piedmont
drainage/landscape contractors**; stand up certification + warranty from day one; then flip to land-
and-expand as win-rate sells the tool.

**Personas/sales motion:** *Homeowner* — reactive, gets 3–4 bids, decides on trust/proof; lead-gen via
local SEO / referrals / Angi / Houzz, front-loaded late-winter→spring; **lead with performance + cost,
sustainability/natural-materials as the closer, the drone+model as the credibility artifact behind a
more convincing bid.** *Sports turf manager* — owns capital budget, buys on rainout-ROI + references;
high-tech framing carries more weight. *Parks/facilities director* — procurement/MS4-driven; here the
**infiltration/recharge benefit becomes the primary purchase driver**.

**Geography:** NC Piedmont Cecil clay first (most-differentiated where infiltration is hardest), then
expand by **soil-analog + metro density** (Upstate SC, N. Georgia, central VA), 1–3 certified
installers per metro before turning on demand.

## 6. Other salient concerns

1. **The core hypothesis is still unvalidated (highest).** The whole wedge rests on "conventional
   drainage underperforms in clay" — and we can't show numbers until the model is built. If field
   data doesn't bear it out clearly, the wedge weakens. *Mitigation: instrument early lighthouse jobs.*
2. **"No gravity outlet" is make-or-break — and clay's low permeability is *why* infiltration is hard**
   (the dry-well failure mode). PIDS must prove engineered preferential pathways beat a saturating dry
   well in exactly that soil.
3. **Two-sided cold-start** (generate demand → seed anchor installers).
4. **Install quality → brand/warranty risk** (hard certification gate; start residential where failures
   are contained, before public fields).
5. **Channel behavior-change** (position the tool as a bid-winner, not a cost).
6. **Low residential ticket** → volume/low-CAC dependent (local SEO/referrals over paid marketplaces;
   sports/municipal carry margin later).
7. **Seasonality** (front-load spring marketing; winter = recruit/train installers).
8. **UIC geometry characterization** + the **no-PE/novel-method legal question** for public work
   (pending the legal opinion flagged in the vertical assessment).

## 7. ALTERNATIVE beachhead — recreational fields · parks · playgrounds

Instead of (or alongside) residential-first, lead with **public/recreational grounds**: municipal &
school athletic/recreational fields, public parks, and **playgrounds**.

- **Buyer / market:** parks-&-rec departments, school-district facilities, athletic associations,
  private rec/sports complexes. **Higher tickets than residential** ($50K–$600K/field; budgeted capital
  + grant-funded), fewer-but-bigger deals.
- **Pain:** ponding closes fields & playgrounds → lost play days, rainouts, mud/safety, turf damage; for
  **playgrounds specifically**, standing water under engineered-wood-fiber / poured-in-place rubber
  surfacing is a recurring **safety + ADA-accessibility + surfacing-degradation** spend.
- **Why it's attractive as the alternative:** (a) **strongest sustainability/GSI narrative fit** — for
  public buyers the *secondary* (infiltration/recharge) and *tertiary* (natural materials) benefits
  become **primary purchase drivers**; (b) **funding tailwinds subsidize it** — MS4/stormwater-credit
  obligations, EPA "GSI in parks," IIJA/IRA stormwater $, and park/rec grants; (c) **flagship-reference
  value** — a public park/field is a visible, citable proof point that residential jobs aren't.
- **Why it's harder (the honest trade):** **procurement-gated** (RFP, fiscal cycles, 6–24 mo — poor
  pre-seed cash cadence); **public work hits the approved-method/PE-stamp/design-manual gate** → needs
  **NC NEST** approval (and this is exactly where the unresolved **no-PE legal question** bites
  hardest); premium fields face sophisticated incumbents (sand-based, Hydraway, SubAir); bonding /
  prevailing-wage overhead.
- **GTM if pursued:** start with **private rec complexes / athletic associations** (single decision-
  maker, faster yes) before public RFP work; lead with rainout-ROI + sustainability; land a
  **grant-funded lighthouse park or playground pilot** for credibility; pursue **NEST in parallel**.
- **Verdict:** a higher-credibility, more-fundable-narrative, sustainability-aligned path — but
  **slower and gated** by approved-method/NEST + procurement. Best as a **credibility/flagship track
  or grant-funded pilot**, *not* the cash-generating first wedge. Residential remains the faster
  proof + cashflow engine; rec/parks/playgrounds is the natural **second leg** (or the lead *iff* a
  grant-funded public pilot materializes and NEST is in motion).

## 8. Adversarial review (2026-06-08) — verdict: would NOT fund as written

A hostile-VC + domain-engineer review. Recorded faithfully; the plan is honest about its risks but
**"honesty has been used as a substitute for resolution."**

**Three kill-shots (each cheaply testable *before* any GTM spend):**
1. **Saturated clay needs a SINK — which is the French-drain outlet problem in disguise.** Engineered
   pathways move water *in* fast, but the clay matrix still gates how fast it's *accepted/dispersed*.
   The plan's own physics concedes wet-season removal "needs somewhere for water to go." Damningly,
   **sand-slit drainage (our named sports-field cousin) works precisely because it drains to a lateral
   pipe outlet** — refuting the "no outlet needed" claim in a flagship segment. *Test (weeks, ~$0): pull
   NRCS SSURGO + NC Geological Survey saprolite/well-log data — is there a reliably present deeper
   higher-K saprolite sink under the Piedmont clay belt? If usually no, the outletless thesis is dead.*
2. **Unauthorized practice of engineering (UPE).** NC Ch. 89C appears to classify subsurface
   storm-drainage system design *with calculations* as the practice of engineering (Class 2
   misdemeanor unlicensed). The "tool, not engineer" posture **weakens precisely because we market the
   output's authority.** *Test (~$3–8K, weeks): a NC construction-law legal opinion — the highest-
   leverage de-risking action, and the plan defers it.*
3. **On-site infiltration may *worsen* the highest-WTP pain.** Infiltrating stormwater into expansive
   clay *near structures* is what foundation experts warn against (shrink-swell heave) — the standard
   fix is to move water *away*. So the loudest residential pain (foundation water) is one PIDS could
   make worse + may be **uninsurable**. *Test (days): interview NC foundation firms + A&E/E&O
   underwriters.*

**Other top vulnerabilities:** (4) **TAM mirage** — the $15B figure is *foundation/structural* damage,
mislabeled to size a *ponding* product; a real bottom-up SAM collapses to a fragmented sliver-of-a-
sliver. (5) **No defensible whitespace** — French drains are cheap/"good enough"; the geometry is prior
art (sand-slit, gravel-mole) trivially copied by ADS, who owns the channel; the real moat (calibrated
model) doesn't exist yet. (6) **Two-sided cold-start through tech-averse contractors by a solo founder**
(83% haven't adopted any AI tool) = five jobs at once; Belgard's lead-lure works because it anchors a
*proven* product. (7) **Unit economics = consulting treadmill** — a ~$5K job's design-fee slice
(~$500–1K) can't cover survey + CAC + E&O + founder time; the segment where economics *do* close
(Tier-3 commercial) is the one blocked by the no-PE gate.

**What must be true (all currently unproven; #1/#2/#3 have evidence pointing the wrong way):** a reliable
deeper sink exists in beachhead clay · PIDS beats French drains by a *provable* margin · the tool posture
survives UPE/design-liability law · infiltration can be sited to avoid heave *without* gutting the ticket
· tech-averse contractors adopt certification+warranty+novel method · a $5K ticket funds the motion.

**Hardening recommendations:** (a) **run the 3 kill-shot tests (<$15K, weeks) BEFORE any GTM dollar**;
(b) stop borrowing the foundation-damage TAM for a ponding product — reposition or commit; (c) **consider
flipping the sequence** — fund the company as an **R&D/credibility engine (SERDP/ESTCP/SBIR + one
grant-funded public lighthouse)** to earn approved-method status + validation data first, residential as
a later *insured* expansion; (d) build the moat as the **clay-calibrated model + outcome-data flywheel**,
never the copyable geometry (commission FTO/prior-art opinion); (e) treat **§7 rec/parks/playgrounds as a
grant-funded flagship, not a cash market**; (f) for the suitability screen, **charge (Tier-3 only) or model
it as CAC**, not residential revenue.
