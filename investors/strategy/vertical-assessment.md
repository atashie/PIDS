## Candidate verticals

1. **Agriculture** — (a) humid/semi-humid growers capturing infiltration-excess runoff (↑ soil
   moisture, ↑ recharge, ↓ nutrient/fertilizer loss); (b) groundwater-dependent regions (e.g. CA
   Central Valley) via Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR); (c) passive/natural sustainability fit.
2. **Grounds drainage** — suburban yards / sports fields / schoolyards / parks; cheaper, natural
   alternative to French drains, catch basins, dry creeks; can send water to deep subsurface (no
   outlet needed → less off-site flooding) + recharge.
3. **Roads / sidewalks** — redirect water ponding below pavement that drives freeze/thaw cracking.
   *(Founder hypothesis: DOT integration too hard for a first vertical — to be tested honestly.)*
4. **Real estate / land development** — replace costly stormwater detention infrastructure without
   taking land (e.g. detention ponds) out of production.
5. **Other / novel** — to be generated and researched.

## The three cross-cutting constraints (true for every vertical)

1. **🪨 Regulatory framing is existential — clean water, BMP not "recharge well."** PIDS's vertical
   tunnels + lateral distribution channels map directly onto EPA's **Class V UIC injection-well**
   definition. In NC, *clean* stormwater infiltration is **authorized by rule (a notification form,
   not a permit)** — but "untreated stormwater shall not be emplaced directly into any aquifer."
   *Discipline:* market PIDS as a **clean-water stormwater infiltration SCM**, never as an "aquifer
   recharge well fed by runoff." The former is a notification; the latter is a permit + open-ended
   contamination liability (and PFAS turns any dirty-runoff vertical into a liability bomb).
2. **📋 The approved-method barrier — sell private sites now, pursue NEST in parallel.** Public/MS4,
   DOT, and NRCS-cost-share work require PIDS to be a *named, credited* method first (NC **NEST** ≈
   12–24 mo, multi-storm monitoring; non-transferable across states). **Private, owner-controlled
   sites need none of that** — an engineer can stamp a site-specific design and build today. So the
   beachhead must be private sites; pursue NEST in parallel to unlock regulated scale.
3. **💧 Soil physics gates the map — but TOWARD clay, not away from it (corrected 2026-06-08, Arik).**
   The research agents (parroting BMP-manual orthodoxy) treated clay as a disqualifier. **That is
   backwards.** PIDS does not rely on native conductivity — it *engineers preferential pathways*
   (coarse-sand channels = macropore analogs + vertical tunnels) that **bypass** the low-K matrix.
   So its value is *highest exactly where native infiltration fails — clay* — which is precisely
   where French drains, infiltration trenches, and dry wells are documented to fail or are disallowed.
   That is a far stronger moat (the only good option on hard sites) than competing with cheap commodity
   solutions on easy sites. Three consequences:
   - **Dry clay is an asset, not a liability.** In summer, clay develops large negative matric
     potential (high suction) → high imbibition rate → PIDS delivers water to dry subsoil *when it's
     most wanted* (growing season). This also revives the humid-clay agricultural soil-moisture case.
   - **Sandy soils are the *worst* target** — adding coarse-sand pathways where the soil is already
     permeable buys little. (Earlier "go to sandy coastal courses" guidance is **withdrawn**.)
   - **The remaining real constraint is a wet-season *sink*.** When clay is saturated its suction +
     uptake collapse, so removal then needs somewhere for water to go — a deeper higher-K layer
     (Piedmont saprolite/transition zone often qualifies), a lateral gravity outlet, or seasonal
     storage that draws down later. That is a **per-site design question the forward-model answers** —
     which is exactly why the model + drone survey *is the product*, not just a check.
   - **Net:** clay-dominated regions — including the home **NC Piedmont** — are a *prime* target.

## GTM model — software-led design + certified-installer network (Arik, 2026-06-08)

A shift in *company shape*: PIDS is not an earthworks contractor but a **software + design + certified-
installer-network** business. The stack:

> **drone / geospatial survey → infiltration-runoff forward-model → optimal PIDS drainage-network map →
> packaged design sold to the site owner → install by pre-vetted, PIDS-certified landscapers.**

Sales pitch: **cheaper + better-performing + high-tech ("drone stuff") + sustainable.**

**Why this is the likely unlock — it fixes the three weaknesses the agents kept hitting:**
- **Channel:** turns landscape/drainage contractors from gatekeeper-adversaries into *distribution*
  partners (resolves the grounds-agent D5 bear case).
- **Defensibility:** the moat becomes the **software + model + certification/brand**, not commodity
  sand & gravel (resolves the D11 weakness).
- **Capital & fundability:** asset-light, high-margin, scalable; "physics-AI + drones for drainage" is
  a genuine deeptech-software pre-seed narrative that plays to the founder's strengths.
- **Vertical-agnostic:** the same stack serves residential, small commercial, institutional grounds,
  vineyards — so vertical choice and GTM model are largely orthogonal.

**Four honest open risks (→ due diligence in progress):**
1. **Revenue model + ticket size.** Mass-residential yard jobs (~$0.5–5k) may be too small to carry a
   separate model-driven design fee. WTP for model-driven design is highest where eyeballing fails —
   **clay sites with expensive, recurring flooding pain** → argues for **premium/prosumer residential +
   small commercial/HOA/institutional** ($10–50k+) over the low-ticket mass tail. *Researching.*
2. **Two-sided cold-start.** Need trained installers *and* owner demand in the same place → go **dense
   and local first (the Triangle/Piedmont)**, which the corrected clay physics also favors.
3. **Quality / liability / brand.** Install quality is existential; a botched "certified" job harms the
   brand and reopens the designer-vs-installer liability/PE-stamp question. Needs a certification + QA +
   warranty program. *Researching the trade-network playbook.*
4. **Model maturity.** Design quality depends on the forward-model (still in build, Module 4) → early
   GTM runs **expert-in-the-loop** with conservative overlays, automating as the model matures.

unsaturated-zone (Richards) model + installer network.** But "drone→3D→CAD" is commoditized
(DroneDeploy/Propeller) and AI is encroaching on drainage design (Bentley OpenSite+, Autodesk
InfoDrainage ML, Trimble WM-Drain for ag tile). **Do NOT pitch "drones + AI" as the innovation** — it
reads as undifferentiated to technical investors. Moat = **(a)** patented primitive (⚠️ crowded patent
field → get a **freedom-to-operate / prior-art opinion**), **(b)** clay-soil calibrated physics (durable
only if fed proprietary field data), **(c)** installer-network outcome-data flywheel (most durable).

**④ Installer network + liability.** Network/QA mechanics = **manageable** (copy Belgard/Unilock: free/
low-fee, audit-gated, recert, lean on an independent competency cert — CMHA/ICPI/NABCEP-style).
**Liability is the real, potentially existential risk.** Authoring drainage designs that affect
foundations/structures/neighbors makes PIDS a **design professional**: likely **PE-stamp required**,
**Spearin** implied warranty (the design author warrants the plans), the **CGL "professional-services
exclusion"** (general liability won't cover design errors → need **A&E E&O**, ~$4–10K+/yr at our
risk profile), and **tort exposure to non-contracting neighbors** that **no limitation-of-liability
clause or installer indemnity can fully shield.** One catastrophic foundation/neighbor-flood claim can
exceed a pre-seed company's entire net worth — the top venture risk.

## Reassessment — "no PE in the loop" (Arik, 2026-06-08) — SUPERSEDES the Tier-3 lead above

Arik: PIDS is scientifically sound but **novel — not part of the civil-engineering toolbox** — so a PE
will not stamp it (a seal stakes the engineer's license on a recognized, defensible method). This
**supersedes the PE-in-responsible-charge mitigation** and reshapes the beachhead:

1. **Permitted/regulated work is gated, not a beachhead.** Anything needing a PE stamp or AHJ approval
   to engineering standards (regulated SCMs, MS4 post-construction, commercial/subdivision site
   stormwater) is **blocked until PIDS earns approved-method status** (NEST etc., multi-year). So
   *permitted* commercial/institutional stormwater is the **post-approval scale market, not the entry
   wedge** — which removes the core appeal of the Tier-3 lead (its "design fee is already a line item"
   only holds for permitted work we cannot yet serve).
2. **Beachhead = private, NON-permitted, owner-decided nuisance/performance drainage** — "standing
   water / soggy field / erosion / wet grounds, fix it" — the domain landscapers, drainage
   contractors, and **growers** operate in *today without a PE*. Revives **(a) residential + private
   grounds** and **(b) agriculture** (grower-decided, private land, no permit, low neighbor/foundation
   liability) as the realistic entry points.
3. **Liability flips: PIDS = the installer's TOOL, not the design author.** Market norm for private
   nuisance work: the contractor designs-and-builds by judgment and carries liability + workmanship
   warranty (as with French drains today). PIDS takes the **software/tool position** (certified
   installer uses PIDS tooling to design+install and stays the responsible party — the
   **Aurora-Solar-to-installers** posture), *not* an external design that shoulders Spearin/design-pro
   liability. Residual exposure → **product liability for the tool/IP** (EULA, installer-judgment-in-
   loop, conservative outputs, disclaimers, insurance), not per-site design liability.
4. **Revenue shifts: owner-paid stamped-design fee → per-project tool license to certified installers**
   (folded into the installer's bid). Lower margin per design, far lower liability, more scalable;
   sidesteps the seat-SaaS "design-cadence" problem (PIDS earns per project). Revisit owner-direct +
   success-fee once a brand/track-record exists.

**Net revised beachhead:** **private, non-permitted nuisance/performance drainage on clay soils —
agriculture (grower-decided) + residential/private grounds — with PIDS as the certified installer's
design tool**, local-first in the NC Piedmont. Permitted commercial/institutional stormwater = the
post-NEST scale market. This is close to Arik's original landscaper-network intuition, plus a
materially upgraded view of agriculture.

## Agricultural deep-dive findings (3 agents, 2026-06-08)

**Row-crop (humid clay): weak first wedge (~2.9/5).** Strong physics fit + an organized installer
channel (LICA drainage contractors) + a nutrient-loss tailwind — but tile already captures the
drainage value (new-install acreage *flat/declining*), the soil-moisture-banking benefit is a
**drought-conditional vitamin** for rain-fed growers (+34% dry years, ~10% normal, *no* significant
soybean gain over 37 yr), 2025 margins are negative-to-thin → capex-averse, and the proof currency is
**multi-year field trials we don't have.** Best (defensive) angle if pursued: NC coastal plain,
lead with **nutrient-loss reduction (~$20–45/ac/yr N + EQIP)**, not drought insurance.

**Specialty / perennial: the genuinely strong ag wedge (~3.4/5) — but a slow design-partner build.**
**Blueberries on heavy/clay sites (MI + NC)** is sharpest: days of waterlogging → root death, drainage
is *already a budgeted Year-0 line item*, market growing (not glutted), **WTP 5–20× row crops**
(~$24K/ac establishment), and the install channel exists consolidated. Adjacencies: hops (Yakima),
expanding Willamette hazelnuts. Wine grapes = great physics, wrong cycle (glut, ~40K ac/yr razed).
**Physics honesty (important):** of PIDS's three effects, **(a) lateral drainage of waterlogged clay
is strongest/well-supported** (gravel-mole-drain precedent); (b) erosion = crowded (cheap cover
crops); **(c) soil-moisture banking is weakest *in clay*** — small pores limit lateral spread, so the
**wetted radius is short → geometry-limited, capex-raising.** *Do not market banking until the
Module-4 sorptive-exchange model produces a defensible wetted-radius number.* Also: gravel-mole drains
are **prior art** → moat must be the design tool, not the buried gravel (reinforces the FTO need).

**Ag GTM / funding / regulatory: ag is NOT the first wedge under the no-PE/tool/cost-share model.**
Three structural blocks: (1) **Funding tailwind is gated** — EQIP/CSP only pay for *codified* NRCS
practice standards; a novel system gets no automatic cost-share, and the only near-term federal money
is **CIG** (pilots, not scale) → multi-year to earn a standard. (2) **Swampbuster trap** — the *same*
USDA lever that would fund adoption can **strip a grower of all USDA benefits if a wet-soil install
clips a wetland**, and PIDS targets exactly the clay/wet soils most likely to be jurisdictional. (3)
**Channel behavior-change with no aligned partner** — the channel is built around perforated pipe +
tile plows + Trimble; nobody sells/pushes sand.

## ⚠️ The single most important unresolved item — the "no-PE" legal question

Removing the PE solves "no one will stamp a novel method" but **raises two unsettled risks the DD flags
as needing a real legal opinion (not web research) before launch:**
1. **Unauthorized practice of engineering (UPE).** In many states, producing *site-specific engineered
   drainage designs* **is** the practice of engineering — so PIDS (or an uncredentialed installer)
   generating buildable designs may be practicing without a license, independent of any tort.
2. **Design-defect liability doesn't disappear — it just loses its licensed bearer.** "We're only a
   tool" erodes as a defense the more prescriptive the output is; plaintiffs sue the deep-pocketed
   design source. The Aurora-style "installer is the responsible designer, PIDS is a tool" posture is
   the intended mitigation but is **unproven, not settled.**

**Action:** commission a **state-specific (start NC) legal opinion** on the no-PE / UPE / design-
liability posture. This gates the whole GTM and belongs in the data room as a named, being-addressed
risk. (Pairs with the **freedom-to-operate / prior-art** opinion the IP-defensibility finding flagged.)
