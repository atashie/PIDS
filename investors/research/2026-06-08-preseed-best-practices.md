## TL;DR for PIDS

- **Instrument:** post-money SAFE, **cap-only, no discount** (≈90% of 2025 pre-seed rounds).
- **Size:** your target band is **$500K–$1.5M**. National pre-seed median ≈ $1M; hardware/deeptech
  skews larger and commands the highest SAFE caps — use that to justify a higher cap rather than
  more dilution.
- **Dilution:** keep total pre-seed (including *stacked* SAFEs) **≤ ~20%**; track cumulative SAFE
  ownership religiously (the post-money SAFE "dilution trap").
- **What substitutes for revenue:** an allowed patent (lead with it as a moat), third-party technical
  validation, and **paid pilots / LOIs** — ranked above waitlists.
- **Funding mix (your choice — all three + local):** run a **parallel stack** — a small equity SAFE
  for go-to-market, **non-dilutive (SBIR/fellowships) for R&D + credibility**, strategic/municipal
  pilots for validation, and the **UNC/Triangle** local ecosystem as a near-term on-ramp.
- **Timing (you chose "prepare for later"):** build strategy + materials now; sequence the actual
  raise behind decisive de-risking milestones (patent grant, validated pilot data). A pre-seed left
  "open" >3 months reads as a failing process — so prep now, *launch compressed* later.
- **Single biggest 2026 risk for our path:** SBIR is congested post-reauthorization — useful, but
  **not** a runway bridge.

## Part 1 — Round Mechanics & Economics

**What "pre-seed" means now.** No standard definition; pre-seed vs. seed is "guidelines, not rules."
Practical 2025–2026 separator: **pre-seed = pre-product/pre-revenue, proving the core risk; seed =
product/early traction exists.** The market moved upmarket — sub-$5M rounds fell from ~62.5% (2015)
to ~33% (2024). ([Carta](https://carta.com/learn/startups/fundraising/pre-seed-funding/),
[Visible.vc](https://visible.vc/blog/startup-funding-stages/))

**Sizes / caps / dilution (Carta 2025 US).** Median pre-seed ≈ **$1M on a post-money SAFE**; caps
~$5–6M (low end) to **$10M** ($250K–$1M rounds) and **$15M** ($1M–$2.5M rounds). Dilution: <$250K →
~5–6%; **$1–2.4M → ~19–20% median** (many >25%). 2026 framing: pre-seed $500K–$2.5M (median ~$1.2M).
([Carta — State of Pre-Seed 2025](https://carta.com/data/state-of-pre-seed-2025/),
[ValueAddVC](https://valueaddvc.com/blog/startup-funding-rounds-in-2025-whats-normal-at-pre-seed-seed-a-and-b),
[capwave.ai](https://capwave.ai/blog/blog-how-much-should-you-raise-pre-seed-2026))

**🟠 Deeptech/hardware:** higher capital need, longer timelines; dedicated deeptech pre-seed funds
write **$250K–$2M** first checks; practical band stretches to ~$1.5M–$4M for capital-intensive
hardware. Hardware/biotech command the **highest SAFE caps** — lean on IP + technical de-risking to
push the cap, not the dilution. ([NfX](https://signal.nfx.com/investor-lists/top-deeptech-pre-seed-investors),
[Carta](https://carta.com/data/state-of-pre-seed-2025/))

**Instrument.** Post-money SAFE is the default (~90% of pre-seed Q1 2025), **cap-only / no discount**
most common. Use the **post-money** YC standard, but beware the **dilution trap**: post-money SAFE
dilution falls on founders/option pool and **stacked SAFEs compound** (e.g., $1.5M across three $500K
SAFEs at a $10M cap ≈ ~15% promised before any priced round). Priced rounds dominate only above ~$5M.
([Carta/YC](https://www.ycombinator.com/documents),
[Horizon Cap — dilution trap](https://horizoncap.vc/founders-guide-the-post-money-safe-dilution-trap/))

**Milestones investors expect (no revenue).** A tightly scoped plan to retire 1–2 decisive
uncertainties with clear pass/fail; a functional prototype an outside expert can validate (the
physics must check out); a credible, quantified commercialization + unit-economics story; LOIs /
design partners / pilots; and **commercialization credibility on the team** (a solo research-only
team is a red flag). ([Amadeus Capital](https://www.amadeuscapital.com/pre-seed-funding-deep-tech-proof-before-revenue/),
[Enduring Planet](https://enduringplanet.com/insights/tips-for-successfully-raising-pre-seed-and-seed-funding-for-your-climate-hardware-startup))

**Timeline.** ~6–10 weeks active raise; ~4–6 months end-to-end with warm intros (+3–4 months if
building the network cold); **add buffer for technical/scientific diligence** in deeptech.
([devops7](https://devops7.com/the-realistic-fundraising-timeline-how-long-it-takes-to-close-pre-seed-and-seed-rounds/))

**Top mistakes.** Raising before ready · dragging the raise (>3 months = bad signal) · overhyping
without evidence / hand-waving the physics · chasing valuation over a value-adding partner · stacked-
SAFE cap-table blindness · raising too little (under-scoped milestone) · **solo-scientist team with
no commercialization credibility.**
([Tran VC](https://www.tran.vc/the-most-common-pre-seed-fundraising-mistakes-and-how-to-avoid-them/))

## Part 2 — Investor Targeting & Sourcing

**Market context.** Capital is concentrating into fewer, larger, higher-conviction checks. **Climate
VC is rebounding and tilting toward hardware/grid/infrastructure** (~$40.5B in 2025). Water is the
smallest but fastest-rising niche (crossed ~$1.12B in 2024).
([Sightline Climate](https://www.sightlineclimate.com/research/40-5bn-and-8-uptick-as-power-demand-drives-25-investment),
[CTVC](https://www.ctvc.co/the-investment-case-for-water/))

**Source taxonomy & fit (high → medium):** angels/syndicates (climate/water domain) · pre-seed/seed
micro-VCs (your primary *lead* source) · climate/water/agtech-thesis funds (best fit, understand long
timelines) · hardware/deeptech funds · accelerators/incubators (pilots + non-dilutive cash + lab
space) · strategics/CVC (great for *pilots*, rarely first-money leads).

**Named starter list (verify before outreach):**
- **Water / infrastructure (closest fit):** Burnt Island Ventures (water-only; $50M Fund II, Oct
  2025), Echo River Capital (~$50K initial checks, water-tech), PureTerra, Emerald Technology
  Ventures. ([ImpactAlpha](https://impactalpha.com/burnt-island-ventures-closes-50m-fund-as-water-tech-draws-fresh-capital/),
  [dww.show](https://dww.show/echo-river-capital-making-waves-in-water-tech-innovation/))
- **Climate (early-stage):** Lowercarbon, Congruent Ventures (incl. built-world & ag), Earthshot,
  MCJ Collective, Voyager, Clean Energy Ventures. ([NfX climate list](https://signal.nfx.com/investor-lists/top-climatetech-cleantech-pre-seed-investors))
- **Hardware/deeptech:** The Engine, Bee Partners, Bolt, Pillar VC, SOSV/HAX.
- **Agtech (for the ag market):** AgFunder, Congruent, Radicle Growth, Omnivore; ag CVCs (Cavallo /
  Wilbur-Ellis, Leaps by Bayer, Syngenta) for field-trial access + distribution.
- **Accelerators (pilots + non-dilutive):** Techstars WaterTech ($20K + $200K SAFE), Elemental
  Excelerator (real-world pilots), Greentown Labs (hardware lab space), Water Innovation Hub
  (ClimateHaven / Xylem). ([xRaise](https://xraise.ai/blog/top-climate-amp-clean-tech-accelerators/))
- **Climate angel syndicates:** Planet Positive (~$200K tickets, hardware-heavy).
- **Databases to pull live, tiered lists:** OpenVC, NfX Signal, vcsheet, Seedtable.

**Process.** Pipeline **~50 investors → ~3 term sheets.** Don't blast — batch **10–12** first
meetings in a tight 1–2 week window to manufacture scarcity/decision pressure.
**Warm intros ≈ 13× the odds of a cold email** — strongest sources are portfolio founders, co-
investors, and **your own pilot-customer CEOs** (a utility/municipality that deploys and then intros
you is the best signal). Run a 3–6 month **"not raising" warm-up tour** before the formal process.
Position as **"climate-critical infrastructure,"** not "climate-aligned feature."
([PMF Show](https://www.pmf.show/blog/how-to-raise-seed-round-fast-fomo-playbook),
[OpenVC warm intros](https://www.openvc.app/blog/warm-intros),
[Holloway](https://www.holloway.com/g/venture-capital/sections/scarcity-and-parallel-fundraising))

## Part 3 — Pitch Materials & Data Room

**Deck (~10–12 slides; engagement drops ~40% past 15).** Title/one-liner · Problem · Solution · Why
Now · Product (highest-attention slide) · Why-You-Win/Moat (IP) · Traction/Validation · Market
(bottom-up) · Business Model · GTM/Competition (don't skip — ~40% of decks fail here) · **Team** (the
biggest 2024 attention shift, esp. at pre-seed) · Ask/Use of Funds (tie to a milestone). Successful
pre-seed decks get ~4 min of viewing; failed ones ~1.5 min — must work on a skim. Narrative is now a
**top investment factor (78% of VCs).**
([YC](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/2u-how-to-build-your-seed-round-pitch-deck),
[DocSend/Dropbox](https://www.dropbox.com/resources/docsend-pitch-deck-research),
[Focused Chaos](https://www.focusedchaos.co/p/i-reviewed-50-startup-pitch-decks))

**🟠 Deeptech deck differences.**
- **Translate the physics for generalists** — if you can't explain PIDS in plain language you land in
  the reject pile. (Acute risk for a Richards-equation/FEM venture.)
- Use **TRL** (and stack **TRL + Manufacturing Readiness + regulatory + GTM on one timeline axis**)
  to show a credible path across the "valley of death." TRL maps to valuation (TRL-3 ≈ heavy
  discount; TRL-6 materially higher). ([Equidam](https://www.equidam.com/deep-tech-startup-valuation-technology-readiness-levels/),
  [FedTech](https://www.fedtech.io/resource/the-readiness-staircase-guide-for-deep-tech-founders))
- **IP as moat, not trophy:** one plain line of claims scope tied to defensibility; depth in an
  appendix. Substitute **paid pilots > LOIs (budget-tied) > waitlist > named pipeline** for revenue.
- Hardware finished 2025 as the **#2 industry by cash raised** — a genuine tailwind for "why now."

**One-pager / cold email.** The one-pager is now the **primary cold-outreach asset** (reportedly
2.5–5× the reply rate of a full deck). Signal-based, personalized outreach pulls ~15–25% replies vs.
~3.4% average. Send the one-pager first; deck for meetings/diligence.
([River](https://rivereditor.com/blogs/write-investor-one-pager-fundraising))

**TAM credibly.** Lead **bottom-up** (defend SAM/SOM), show methodology, don't mix methods. A
defensible $500M SOM beats a fuzzy $50B TAM. Seed VCs want TAM $1B+ / defensible SAM $200–500M.
([Whitepage](https://www.whitepage.studio/blog/market-size-slide-pitch-deck))

**Data room (keep tight: ~8–12 docs).** Deck + one-pager · **simple 12–18 mo** financial model
(burn/runway/assumptions) · **clean cap table incl. SAFEs** (missing = red flag) · incorporation docs
· validation evidence (interviews, LOIs, pilot results) · market/competitor overview · **[deeptech]**
organized technical docs + **IP/patent filings**. **Premature/omit:** to-the-penny 3–5 yr model,
hockey-stick projections, over-stuffing. The real pre-seed test: how you *think about spending the
money* (itemized use of funds). ([RaiseTalks](https://raisetalks.com/blog/data-room-checklist-pre-seed-startups-2025/),
[Pitchdrive](https://www.pitchdrive.com/academy/building-a-pre-seed-data-room-what-to-include-and-why-it-matters-for-early-stage-fundraising))

## Part 4 — Non-Dilutive & Deeptech-Specific Funding

> **⏰ Critical timing:** SBIR/STTR **lapsed Oct 1, 2025** and was **reauthorized Apr 13, 2026**
> (now extended through Sept 30, 2031) after the longest shutdown in the program's history. Restart
> is staggered and **congested** through 2026 (DoD/NASA first; NSF/DOE/USDA/EPA on varying timelines).
> **Treat SBIR as a 9–18 month play, not a runway bridge.**
> ([Crowell & Moring](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/sbirsttr-programs-reauthorized-after-six-month-lapse),
> [SSTI](https://ssti.org/blog/sbir-slowly-relaunching-following-presidents-signature))

**SBIR/STTR agency fit for PIDS:** **NSF** (America's Seed Fund; Phase I ~$305K, Phase II ~$1M),
**USDA-NIFA** (ag water mgmt / precision ag; Phase I ~$175K, Phase II ~$600K), **EPA** (stormwater /
clean water; SBIR-only), **DoT** (roads/sidewalks drainage directly in scope), DOE (water-energy
nexus), NOAA (hydrology/flood, niche). STTR requires a research-institution partner (≥30%) — a path
to plug in a university hydrology/modeling lab. Catch: slow, effort-heavy (an NSF Phase I app ≈
80–120 founder-hours), R&D-scoped (not GTM/sales).
([NSF SeedFund](https://seedfund.nsf.gov/our-program/),
[NIFA](https://www.nifa.usda.gov/grants/programs/sbir-sttr/program-information),
[SLED.AI](https://www.sledai.com/blog/sbir-phase-1-vs-phase-2/))

**Deeptech fellowships (highest-fit, IP-preserving):** **Activate Fellowship** (2 yr; ~$80–110K/yr
stipend + ~$100K facility support; explicit climatetech; non-dilutive) and **DOE LEEP** (Cyclotron
Road / Chain Reaction / Innovation Crossroads / West Gate — 2 yr funded fellowship, lab access, R&D
grant; >$1B alumni follow-on). Water/stormwater/ag-water-nexus framing fits the climate mandate.
([Activate](https://activate.org/the-fellowship),
[DOE LEEP](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ammto/lab-embedded-entrepreneurship-program))

**Climate/water non-dilutive:** Elemental Impact (equity-free $350K–$1M; pioneered the
"Development-SAFE" for first projects), Greentown Labs (up to $50K non-dilutive), Third Derivative;
EPA challenges/prizes. **Public water-infrastructure money** (Clean Water State Revolving Fund;
EPA stormwater grants; state water boards) is best framed as **demand-side / pilot funding routed
through municipal customers**, not company R&D capital — design pilots a city/county can pay for with
these dollars. ([xRaise](https://xraise.ai/blog/top-climate-amp-clean-tech-accelerators/),
[Latitude Media](https://www.latitudemedia.com/news/this-tool-gives-non-dilutive-financing-to-climate-tech-foaks/),
[Funding Landscape](https://fundinglandscape.com/answers/water-infrastructure-grants-funding-2026))

**Grant ↔ equity interaction.** Winning SBIR (esp. Phase II) is **elite third-party validation** that
de-risks IP for VCs; one cited case: $250K Phase I → prototype → $1.2M seed at ~4× the valuation. A
**post-money SAFE runs in parallel** with grant applications (SAFEs don't set price/board, so no
conflict). For climate hardware, the eventual full stack is **equity + grants + debt + project
finance + offtake**; FOAK/deployment financing is deferred to post-traction.
([KJK](https://kjk.com/2026/04/10/once-more-into-the-valley-of-death-navigating-sbir-sttr-funding-for-tech-startups-after-2025/),
[Gigascale](https://gigascale.com/insights/capital-stack-pre-seed-seed-climate-companies/))

**Equity-adjacent:** university tech-transfer / venture studios (co-creation model, dovetails with
STTR); dual-use/defense (DIU, AFWERX STRATFI/TACFI) is credible given drone-mapping + terrain/water
modeling — but a real **roadmap-distraction risk** for a civil-infrastructure company; hold as
optional upside.

## Part 5 — What This Means for PIDS (synthesis)

1. **Sequence, don't pick.** You chose equity + non-dilutive + strategic/pilots. The literature
   strongly favors a **parallel stack**: small equity SAFE (GTM/team/pilots) ∥ non-dilutive
   (Activate/LEEP + SBIR for R&D + credibility) ∥ municipal/strategic pilots (validation + warm-intro
   engine). They reinforce each other; a grant win raises the equity valuation.
2. **"Prepare for later" is the right posture — design the milestone trigger.** The decisive
   de-risking events (patent *granted*, a validated field pilot, third-party technical sign-off) are
   what convert this into a fast, compressed raise. Build the materials now; pull the trigger when a
   milestone lands, then run a tight 6–10 week process.
3. **Lead with the moat + the physics-made-simple.** The allowed patent + the rigorous forward-model
   validation are our differentiators — but must be translated out of FEM/Richards jargon. A
   TRL/MRL-on-one-axis roadmap turns the science story into a fundable de-risking narrative.
4. **The team-credibility red flag is the one to confront early.** A science-heavy founder needs a
   visible commercialization counterweight (co-founder/advisor) before or during the raise.
5. **Local first (Triangle/UNC).** As a UNC alum in Chapel Hill, the local ecosystem (NC IDEA,
   Innovate Carolina, Triangle angels) is the cheapest warm-intro + non-dilutive on-ramp — see the
   appended local-ecosystem research.
