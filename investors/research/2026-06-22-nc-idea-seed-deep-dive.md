## TL;DR / recommendation

- **PIDS is clearly eligible** and clears every hard + soft screen *except the traction bar*. The allowed
  **patent is a real asset** — the application has a dedicated **DEFENSIBILITY** + **PATENT DETAILS** field.
- **The one real gap is demonstrated market traction.** NC IDEA's own recipients: *"Don't go for the SEED
  unless you already have some evidence of market traction."* SEED expects an **MVP + market readiness +
  traction (esp. last 6 months).**
- **Decision rule for Fall 2026 (you may apply to only ONE of SEED/MICRO per cycle):**
  - **→ SEED ($50K)** if, by the **Aug 24, 2026** deadline, PIDS can show a working MVP + concrete
    market-readiness evidence (LOIs, design partners, pilot interest, paid pilots, strong customer-discovery).
  - **→ MICRO ($10K)** if traction is still thin — frame it as a funded **customer-discovery experiment**
    (NC IDEA literally prescribes this), then be *"competitive for SEED within 2 years."*
- **Do now regardless:** attend a **July info session/webinar**, use NC IDEA **virtual office hours**, draft
  early (don't wait for the late-July portal open), and line up **US incorporation** (LLC ok) so it can't
  block disbursement.

## 1. Fall 2026 timeline  [C — live SEED page]

A **~16-week, 4-gate funnel** from open to award.

| Date (2026) | Stage |
|---|---|
| **Jul 13** | Grants Info **Webinar** (virtual) |
| Jul 14–22 | Regional info sessions (Charlotte 14 · Asheville 15 · Wilmington 20 · **Triangle 21** · Triad 22) |
| **Jul 27** | **Application opens** |
| **Aug 24** | **Application deadline — 5:00 pm EST** |
| **Sep 18** | Semi-finalists selected (~**20–24**) |
| **Sep 28** | Semi-finalist revised application due (+ a **15-min phone interview**) |
| **Oct 23** | Finalists selected (~**10–12**) |
| Oct 29 – Nov 2 | Finalist pitch-scrub sessions |
| **Nov 6** | **Finalist pitches** (live, before **35–40 reviewers**) |
| **Nov 11** | **Recipients selected (~5–7)** |
| Dec 2026 – 2027 | Funds released in tranches (Fall cycle: first payment **late Dec/early Jan** [T]) |

*Info-session dates were "TBD" earlier in the cycle; reconfirm closer to July.* Funnel: all applicants →
~20–24 → ~10–12 → ~5–7. Program total since 2006: **>$10M to 219 companies** [C].

## 2. Eligibility (hard rules + soft screens)

**Hard / structural** —
- **Geography:** *"headquartered in and … conduct a majority of corporate operations in … North Carolina"* [C];
  all public-facing materials should identify the business as an NC company [T]; **must maintain majority of
  ops in NC for ≥12 months after grant completion** [C]. ("Majority of operations" is **not numerically
  defined** [?].)
- **Founder:** **≥1 full-time founder living in NC.** Full-time = *"works 40+ hours a week on their startup
  and does not spend more than 15 hours a week"* on another job, another of the founder's startups, or
  **school (incl. class + homework)** [C/T]. (All founders in NC is *preferred, not required* [C].)
- **Entity:** may **apply** un-incorporated, but must be **US-incorporated (LLC counts; need NOT be NC)
  before any funds are released** [C].
- **Ineligible categories** [T]: illegal-in-NC businesses; **high-capital/long-runway (e.g. pharma)**;
  consulting/agencies; distributors; event-venue mgmt; franchises; lifestyle/service (restaurants, retail,
  catering, cleaning); nonprofits; resellers; TV/film; traditional publishing; staffing agencies; training
  companies; trucking/freight. **→ PIDS (hardware deeptech) is not on this list.**

**Soft screens ("less likely to be considered," not hard cutoffs)** —
- Trailing-12-mo **revenue > $250K** ($500K for food & beverage) [C]. *PIDS pre-revenue → clear.*
- **> $250K equity** invested OR **> $1M non-dilutive** received at time of applying [C]. Founder + friends-
  &-family funding does **not** count against you [C]. *PIDS no outside equity → clear.*

**IP / scalability gates** —
- **Proprietary IP or sustained competitive advantage** required [C] → **PIDS's allowed patent satisfies this.**
- **Scalable:** *"a clear path to scale — either … venture capital within two years or by achieving $2M+ in
  annual revenue within five years with potential to exceed $10M+"* [C].
- **Proof of concept** = *"existence of a problem, market readiness and an MVP"* + *"prepared to leverage a
  $50K grant to scale"* [C].

## 3. The application (Spring 2025 template — Fall 2026 may shift slightly) [T]

Online only (NC IDEA Community Portal); the template is for offline drafting/char-counting. **Video is
OPTIONAL** (≤3 min, one URL; "highly encouraged for software companies"). Tabs + key free-form fields:

- **Company Information** — eligibility attestation; structure; incorporation date; NC county; business
  category/sub-category; target market; **University Affiliation** (asked, *not* a gate); advisors (800);
  supporting communities (800); previous-applicant + "what measurably changed" (750).
- **Founders** — per founder: bio relevance, FT/PT status (+ milestone to go FT), NC residency, **university
  affiliation (current/historical student/faculty/employee)**, demographics. *(LinkedIn must match the form.)*
- **Staff (non-founder)** — per person bio/role.
- **Business Details** — **Elevator pitch (500)** · **Problem (1,500)** · **Solution (2,000)** ·
  **Defensibility in the marketplace (1,500)** · **Patent details (800)** · **Risk: riskiest assumption (1,000)**.
- **Customer & Market** — **Customer discovery (1,200)** ("how many potential customers have you talked to…
  what evidence") · Customer description (1,500) · **Customer acquisition / GTM (2,000)** · Revenue model
  (2,000) · **Market opportunity $ at launch & scale, with sources (2,000)** · Competition (3,000).
- **Traction** — *"progress … specifically in the past 6 months that indicates market traction"* (2,000) +
  pre-revenue/revenue figures.
- **Equity Investment** + **Additional Funding** — fundraising status, equity history, founder/grant/loan/
  gift/award amounts, how funds were used.
- **Grant Request** — **Impact (500)** ("what goal(s) will the $50K help you achieve … to extend runway") ·
  **Milestones: 3–8 specific & measurable over the ~6–10 month grant period (1,200)** · **Use of funds (1,200)**.

**Review:** by *"the NC IDEA team, investors and other partners"* [C]. Reviewers weight: a **categorical
stage-change** the $50K enables; **customer/purchase proof points**; **plain language (avoid jargon)**; and —
because finalists pitch live to 35–40 reviewers — **the founder, not just the idea**. Common misses: applying
to SEED **too early/without traction**; rushing; jargon; a use-of-funds plan that doesn't change the company's
stage [C/I].

## 4. Use of funds + post-award

- **Eligible spend** [C]: customer discovery; website/app dev; **prototype development**; marketing; **legal
  incl. IP/patent costs**; business development incl. travel/trade shows; **consultant fees; staff salaries.**
  *(Hardware-favorable — prototype + patent costs + salaries all eligible.)*
- **Timing:** only expenses **after the grant agreement is signed** (no pre-award reimbursement) [C].
- **Disbursement:** **$50K in 4 milestone-based tranches over 6–12 months**, each released after a progress
  report; schedule agreed post-award [T/C].
- **Non-dilutive** — **no equity, royalty, or repayment** stated [C]. The one binding ongoing obligation is
  the **12-month NC-operations covenant** [C]. *(No explicit clawback language found — read the grant
  agreement [?].)*
- **Follow-on:** mentorship, monthly seminars, intros to funders/partners; recipients announced at the annual
  Ecosystem Summit [C]. *(No primary-confirmed automatic downstream investment fund tied to SEED [?].)*

## 5. MICRO vs SEED  [C]

| | MICRO ($10K) | SEED ($50K) |
|---|---|---|
| Stage | proof-of-concept *in progress* | proof-of-concept *demonstrated*, ready to scale |
| Scalability | VC ≤4 yr OR $2M ≤7 yr | VC ≤2 yr OR $2M ≤5 yr ($10M+ potential) |
| FT founder | within 6–12 months | now |
| Best when | *"cool idea but no customers yet"* → run a $10K customer-discovery experiment | *"already have some evidence of market traction"* |

**You may apply to only one per cycle** [C]. The pivot is **traction**, not whether the model is finished.
