**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Scope:** The **faculty-partnership funding lever** at four universities where the founder has existing
professor relationships — **Virginia Tech**, **University of Virginia**, **Southern Methodist University

from [`2026-06-08-unc-triangle-ecosystem.md`](2026-06-08-unc-triangle-ecosystem.md): *attaching a
university faculty co-PI / research partner unlocks state + university money that is otherwise closed to a

## ⭐ Keystone insight & ranking

The UNC pattern — *a faculty co-PI unlocks (1) a state applied-research fund private companies can't touch,
(2) university internal gap/POC money, (3) federal **STTR** eligibility (statutorily requires a research
institution, **the same everywhere**), and (4) a state 50%-match on a federal SBIR/STTR award* — **repeats,
but with sharply different strength** at each school:

| Rank | School (state) | NCInnovation analog (state R&D, faculty-gated) | One-NC analog (state SBIR/STTR match) | Standout extra | Funding-lever strength |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **U. Alabama** (AL) | **ARDEF** (ADECA) — university-paired R&D, ~$100K–$367K | ✅ **Confirmed** — Innovate Alabama Supplemental: 50% up to **$100K (Ph I) / $250K (Ph II)** | **Alabama Water Institute + NOAA CIROH** — the best hydrology research home in any of these states | **Strongest for PIDS** |
| **2** | **Virginia Tech + UVA** (VA) | **CCF Higher-Ed Proof-of-Concept** (VIPC) — university-only applicant, **$75K–$300K** | ✅ VIPC SBIR/STTR match (~$100K, *snippet*) | **UVA LVG Seed Fund (~$10M)** equity vehicle + deep water/stormwater faculty | **Strong, mature (two schools = two bites)** |
| **3** | **SMU** (TX) | ✗ none reaches a startup (state research funds are **public-university-only**; SMU is private) | ✗ **Texas has no statewide match** (key negative) | Strong *personal* relationship + a specific faculty fit (Smits, MAR/vadose-zone) | **Weakest as a funding lever** |

**Bottom line:** **Alabama and Virginia are real, fundable analogs to the UNC opportunity; Texas/SMU is
not** (leverage the SMU relationship for STTR + Dallas pilots + a named PI, but expect little *state* money —
a Texas **public** university would be a far stronger funding partner). Federal **STTR** is the one
universal faculty gate that pays off at every school.

## Virginia (Virginia Tech + UVA)

**⭐ Keystone.** Virginia's state commercialization fund — **VIPC's Commonwealth Commercialization Fund
(CCF**, the merged successor to the old CRCF + Virginia Research Innovation Fund as of 2020-07-01) — runs a
**Higher-Education Proof-of-Concept (HE POC)** track whose *applicant must be a Virginia university, its IP
foundation, or a VA nonprofit research institute* — **a for-profit founder cannot apply at all.** Tied to
that, each university's own gap money (VT's LAUNCH POC, UVA's LVG Seed Fund) requires a university IP
disclosure/license and a faculty/student inventor. A VT or UVA faculty co-PI unlocks all of it.

### State-level programs
1. **CCF — Higher-Ed Proof-of-Concept (HE POC)** — *closest NCInnovation analog; fully faculty-gated.*
   Track 1 ≤**$75K**; Track 2 ≤**$150K**; discretionary Advanced Commercialization ≤**$300K**; 1:1 match
   required. **Applicant must be a VA university / IP foundation / nonprofit research institute — not a
   private company.** Two cycles/yr (spring 2026 closed Apr 30; next ~fall 2026).
   [vipc.org/funding/he-poc](https://vipc.org/funding/he-poc/)
2. **CCF — Private-Sector track** — *founder-direct, but VA-residency-gated.* **$100K–$300K** across three
   TRL tiers. Requires a for-profit **HQ'd in Virginia**, ≥50% senior management VA-resident, ≥50% founder
   ownership held by VA residents, ≤1 prior VIPC grant. The VA-domicile tests are decisive if PIDS isn't VA-based.
   [vipc.org/funding/commonwealth-commercialization-fund](https://vipc.org/funding/commonwealth-commercialization-fund/)
3. **VIPC SBIR/STTR support — FFAP + "Phase 0" match** — *One-NC analog; founder-direct.* Up to ~**$3K**
   Phase-0 prep + up to ~**$100K** post-award match for first-time Phase I/II (**both figures snippet-only —
   the live VIPC page lists services, not dollars; confirm with VIPC**). STTR encouraged (and STTR itself
   requires a research partner). [vipc.org/initiatives/sbir-sttr](https://vipc.org/initiatives/sbir-sttr/)
4. **VIPC Launch grants & Virginia Venture Partners (VVP)** — *founder-direct.* Launch = non-dilutive grants
   + convertible notes (pre-MVP); VVP = pre-seed **$250K**, seed ≤**$1M** equity, VA-based.
   [vipc.org/funding/startups](https://vipc.org/funding/startups/)
5. **VIPC Lab-to-Launch** — *IP-gated, ≤$50K* to reimburse licensing/commercialization costs; requires the
   Fast-Track License with a VA R1 university (UVA, **Virginia Tech**, VCU, GMU, ODU, W&M).
   [vipc.org/funding/lab-to-launch](https://vipc.org/funding/lab-to-launch/)
6. **GO Virginia** regional growth grants — funds *public/nonprofit orgs only* (≥2 localities, 2:1), so PIDS
   benefits only *through* a VT/UVA-led project. [dhcd.virginia.gov/gova](https://www.dhcd.virginia.gov/gova)
7. **Virginia Catalyst (VBHRC)** — **$200K–$800K** but **life-sciences/human-health ONLY** → PIDS out of
   scope; listed for completeness. [virginiacatalyst.org](https://www.virginiacatalyst.org/)

### Virginia Tech internal
- **LAUNCH Proof-of-Concept Grant** (via **VTIP**) — ≤**$50K**; PI need not be faculty but **tech must be
  disclosed to VTIP** first (+ NSF I-Corps short course). FY26 due **Jan 23, 2026**.
  [vt.edu/link/.../poc-grant-program](https://www.vt.edu/link/innovation/programs/poc-grant-program.html)
- **VTIP** (tech-transfer entity, gateway to all VT IP money) · **LINK + LICENSE + LAUNCH** hub
  [link.vt.edu/innovation/funding](https://link.vt.edu/innovation/funding.html)
- **VT KnowledgeWorks** incubator (in the for-profit **Corporate Research Center**); annual Tech Transfer
  Challenge (~$100K in mentorship/services). [vtknowledgeworks.com](https://www.vtknowledgeworks.com/)

### UVA internal
- **UVA LVG Seed Fund (I & II)** — **~$10M** total, managed by **Licensing & Ventures Group**; equity into
  companies commercializing UVA IP or founded by UVA faculty/staff/students/i.Lab cos. *(Per-company check
  size unpublished — verify with LVG.)* The standout university-affiliated venture vehicle (NC has no
  equivalent). [lvg.virginia.edu/funding/uva-lvg-seed-fund](https://lvg.virginia.edu/funding/uva-lvg-seed-fund)
- **UVA i.Lab Incubator** (Darden Batten) — ~$5K base/team, open to all UVA students/alumni/faculty.
- *(UVA's other translational/gap funds — Coulter, Ivy Biomedical, etc. — are biomedical-only; out of scope.)*

### Best-matched academic partners

## Texas (SMU)

**⭐ Keystone — and an honest caveat.** The UNC model **does not replicate in Texas.** Texas's research
endowments (Texas University Fund, NRUF) fund *public universities directly* and never reach a startup; the
nearest-in-spirit program (**GURI**) is a faculty-*recruitment* match for *public* institutions, not a
commercialization grant; and **Texas has no statewide SBIR/STTR match at all** (the One-NC analog is
*absent* — cash match exists only at the **City of San Antonio**, not Dallas). SMU is **private**, so it is
*ineligible* for the flagship state research funds, and its internal gap funding is thin. What an SMU faculty
partnership *does* unlock: **federal STTR eligibility** (the universal statutory gate), a **named PI +
research credibility** for USDA/NSF/EPA proposals and TWDB aquifer-recharge municipal pilots, and Dallas-
region access. **A Texas *public* university (UT-Austin, Texas A&M) would be a materially stronger *funding*
partner** — leverage SMU for the relationship + specific faculty fit, and consider a public co-PI if the goal
is maximizing state/federal research dollars.

### State-level programs (mostly closed to PIDS)
1. **Texas University Fund / NRUF** — public-university research endowments; **SMU ineligible (private)**;
   never reach a startup. [highered.texas.gov](https://www.highered.texas.gov/research-funding-in-texas/research-funding-and-programs/)
2. **GURI** — ≤$5M faculty-recruitment match, **public institutions only**; not a startup grant.
   [gov.texas.gov/business/page/guri](https://gov.texas.gov/business/page/guri)
3. **Product Development & Small Business Incubator Fund (PDSBIF)** — ≤$5M **loans** (not grants); requires
   ≥3 yr operating history + ≥1 yr revenue, and **"R&D-phase products are ineligible"** → revisit post-revenue.
   [gov.texas.gov/.../pdsbi](https://gov.texas.gov/business/page/product-development-and-small-business-incubator-fund)
4. **Texas Enterprise Fund** — deal-closing job grants (75+ jobs); not a fit for a pre-revenue startup.
5. **❌ State SBIR/STTR match — DOES NOT EXIST** (SSTI guide; key negative). Cash match only at **City of
   San Antonio** (Ph0 ≤$2K / Ph I ≤$50K / Ph II ≤$75K, SA-HQ only). No Dallas equivalent.
   [ssti.org/state-sbirsttr-resource-guide](https://ssti.org/state-sbirsttr-resource-guide)
6. **CPRIT** — funds for-profit product dev, but **cancer-only** → out of scope.
7. **Texas Water Fund / TWDB ASR-MAR programs** *(most PIDS-relevant state money)* — Prop 6 + SB7/HB500 put
   ~$2.5B+ into water supply incl. **aquifer storage & recovery / recharge**, **but funds flow to
   municipalities & groundwater-conservation districts, not private startups** → for PIDS this is a **pilot /
   customer / co-applicant channel**, not a company grant. A faculty PI + municipal partner strengthens it.
   [twdb.texas.gov/.../asr](https://www.twdb.texas.gov/innovativewater/asr/index.asp)

### SMU internal (thin)
- **Office of Technology Transfer & Commercialization** — licenses SMU IP; **no published POC/gap-fund
  dollar amounts** (faculty/SMU-IP-gated — the KickStart analog, but unfunded as far as public pages show).
  [smu.edu/provost/research/tech-transfer](https://www.smu.edu/provost/research/tech-transfer)
- **Caruth Institute for Entrepreneurship (Cox)** — Business Accelerator: semifinalist pre-seed ≤**$10K**,
  finalist "SMU Startup Launch" ≤**$50K** cash + in-kind (largely student/affiliate-gated, small-dollar).
  [smu.edu/cox/.../caruth-institute](https://www.smu.edu/cox/centers-institutes/caruth-institute-for-entrepreneurship)
- *Regional:* **Texas Research Alliance** (N-TX university↔industry connector incl. SMU — credibility, not
  grants); **Capital Factory** All Access Fund (~$100K/≤1% equity, founder-direct).

### Best-matched academic partners
- **SMU Lyle — Civil & Environmental Engineering: Dr. Kathleen Smits, P.E. (CEE Chair).** Strongest named fit:
  published work on **managed aquifer recharge (MAR) + vadose-zone soil-water/gas transport**, NSF CAREER,
  vadose-zone/hydrology journal editor. *Caveat:* her MAR/vadose work leans energy/gas-transport, not
  surface-water drainage — but the **subsurface unsaturated-flow + Ag-MAR overlap with PIDS's recharge
  vertical is direct**, and a sitting chair is a high-value PI. CEE has subsurface fate/transport modeling +
  a Geotech/Hydraulics/Hydrology lab. [people.smu.edu/ksmits](https://people.smu.edu/ksmits/publications/)
- **Stronger public alternatives (strategic note):** UT-Austin (Jackson School hydrogeology / Bureau of
  Economic Geology; POC awards ≤~$125K), Texas A&M (Water Mgmt & Hydrologic Science; ~$5M ADM gap fund) —
  deeper sponsored-research infrastructure + state-fund eligibility SMU structurally lacks.

## Alabama (University of Alabama, Tuscaloosa)

**⭐ Keystone.** A UA partnership unlocks **two** state money streams *and* the best PIDS science home of any
of these states: (1) the **ADECA Alabama R&D Enhancement Fund (ARDEF)** — Alabama's NCInnovation analog,
built around an *"eligible research entity" (university) paired with a "private sector applicant"*, with
awards routed to/through the research institution; and (2) the **Innovate Alabama Supplemental Grant** — a
**confirmed** One-NC analog paying a **state 50% match on a federal SBIR/STTR award**. On top of that, UA
hosts the **Alabama Water Institute + NOAA's CIROH** — a national water-prediction hub co-located with the
**NOAA National Water Center** — arguably the single most credible research home for a passive-infiltration /
aquifer-recharge venture in any of these states.

### State-level programs
1. **ARDEF (ADECA)** — *NCInnovation analog; faculty-gated.* Recent UA awards ~**$100K–$367K** (FY24/25 round
   $1.6M across UA/Auburn/Southern Research/HudsonAlpha). University research-entity + private-applicant
   pairing; **50% match required, state funds can't count as match** (the university can supply the match);
   the private partner **may be HQ'd outside AL**. FY27 deadline **Jul 29, 2026**. *(Mandatory-partnership
   wording inferred from program structure + award history — verify in the Program Guide.)*
   [adeca.alabama.gov/ardef](https://adeca.alabama.gov/ardef/)
2. **Innovate Alabama Supplemental Grant** — *One-NC analog, CONFIRMED state SBIR/STTR match; founder-direct.*
   **Ph I → 50% up to $100K; Ph II → 50% up to $250K**, non-dilutive (>$17M to 82 businesses to date).
   Requires an active federal SBIR/STTR award — and the upstream **STTR statutorily requires the UA research
   partner**, so the faculty tie is the gate that makes this match reachable.
   [innovatealabama.org/programs/supplemental-grant-program](https://innovatealabama.org/programs/supplemental-grant-program/)
3. **Alabama Launchpad (EDPA + Innovate Alabama)** — *competitive non-dilutive; founder-direct.* Technology
   track ≤**$75K** (Life Sci ≤$100K, Consumer ≤$50K) + $2,500 to finalists + 12-wk mentorship; two cycles/yr.
   [alabamalaunchpad.com](https://alabamalaunchpad.com/)
4. **Innovate Alabama Capital Access Fund** — *equity, not a grant; founder-direct.* **$15M** fund, ~$100K–
   $250K/company, ≥1:1 private match; prioritizes Commerce "CatALyst" sectors (construction-tech adjacent).
   [innovatealabama.org/programs/capital-access-fund](https://innovatealabama.org/programs/capital-access-fund/)
5. **Alabama Futures Fund** — Alabama-focused private seed VC (dilutive). [alabamafuturesfund.com](https://alabamafuturesfund.com/)

### UA internal (Tuscaloosa)
- **UA Office for Innovation & Commercialization (OIC) / Tech Development** — commercializes UA IP (licensing
  or startup); runs **Innovate UA** (NSF Accelerating Research Translation–funded). **Faculty/UA-IP-gated.**
  [research.ua.edu/offices/innovation-commercialization](https://research.ua.edu/offices/innovation-commercialization/)
- **The EDGE (Tuscaloosa)** — $11.7M UA + City + Chamber incubator (founder-direct). **EDGE Labs** (fka Bama
  Technology Incubator) — on-campus wet/dry prototyping labs (faculty-leaning). **Alabama Entrepreneurship
  Institute** + **Techstars Tuscaloosa** (accelerator, equity). [aei.culverhouse.ua.edu/the-edge](https://aei.culverhouse.ua.edu/the-edge/)
- *(No named, dollar-quantified UA standalone POC/gap fund confirmed — verify with OIC.)*

### Best-matched academic partners — the PIDS standout
- **Alabama Water Institute (AWI)** — **$34M FY24 extramural / 57 grants**; flagship water-research hub and
  the natural institutional home. [awi.ua.edu/about](https://awi.ua.edu/about/)
- **CIROH (NOAA Cooperative Institute, UA-led)** — ~**$25.5M / 47 projects** recent cycle; co-located with the
  **NOAA National Water Center + USGS Hydrologic Instrumentation Facility**; **admits private-sector
  consortium members** (a possible subaward/partnership path). *Caveat:* CIROH's headline themes skew to
  streamflow/flood forecasting — **groundwater/infiltration/recharge scope-fit needs PI-level confirmation.**
  [ciroh.ua.edu](https://ciroh.ua.edu/)
- **Depts. of Geological Sciences** and **Civil, Construction & Environmental Engineering** — likely home
  departments for a hydrogeology / vadose-zone / Richards-equation / drainage-engineering co-PI; both feed AWI.
- *Read:* the highest-leverage play is the **STTR → Innovate Alabama Supplemental Grant stack** (UA faculty
  STTR partner satisfies the federal requirement *and* unlocks the 50% state match ≤$250K) **+ ARDEF** for
  direct collaborative R&D; the **AWI/CIROH affiliation** is the credibility multiplier on both.

## What this means for the fundraising plan

This enriches **Track 2, Lane A (university-partnered non-dilutive)** in
[`../strategy/fundraising-plan.md`](../strategy/fundraising-plan.md): the founder now has **four named
faculty options across three states**, not just the UNC/NC State path. Practical reads:

- **The university-partner track is a portfolio, not a single bet.** Pursue the relationship(s) with the
  strongest *combined* science-fit + funding-stack: **UA (ARDEF + confirmed state SBIR/STTR match + AWI/CIROH)
  and Virginia (CCF HE-POC + VIPC match + UVA Seed Fund + Sample/Sridhar/Goodall)** are the two real analogs
  to the UNC opportunity.
- **Federal STTR is the common denominator.** Every one of these faculty ties satisfies STTR's statutory
  research-partner requirement — and in **AL and (pending verification) VA** that STTR award then triggers a
  **state 50% match**. STTR is the cleanest universal payoff of any professor relationship.
- **SMU/Texas: keep, but reframe.** Use the SMU relationship for **STTR + a named PI (Smits) + Dallas/TWDB
  municipal recharge pilots**, not for state grant money. If Texas becomes a priority geography, a **public**
  co-PI is the stronger funding move.
- **Most state R&D money (ARDEF, CCF HE-POC, NCInnovation) is *faculty-gated* — reachable only through the
  professor.** That is exactly the lever this venture is positioned to pull, and it argues for locking in
  one or two research partners early (consistent with the UNC analysis's "keystone move").
- **Caution:** several programs carry **in-state residency/HQ tests** (VA CCF private-sector; some AL/state
  matches) — PIDS's state of domicile will materially affect which doors open. Worth deciding deliberately.
