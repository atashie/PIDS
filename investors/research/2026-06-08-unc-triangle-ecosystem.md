## ⭐ Keystone insight

The **richest local non-dilutive money** (UNC KickStart, NCInnovation) is gated to **UNC-System
faculty / research-affiliated ventures, not alumni-as-such.** The highest-leverage move is to
**attach a UNC or NC State researcher as a co-PI / scientific partner** — a natural fit given our 3D
hydrology + drainage physics. That single move simultaneously unlocks:
1. UNC **KickStart / OTC translational** money (if partner is UNC + IP licensed),
2. **NCInnovation** (large TRL 3–6 grants, on the research side),
3. the federal **STTR** path (which *requires* a research-institution partner),
4. **One NC** 50% state match on the resulting federal award.

Meanwhile **NC IDEA SEED/MICRO + Launch Chapel Hill + One NC + Triangle angels** are open to the
founder **directly, with no university tie.**

**Best-matched academic partner: NC State Stormwater Engineering Group (Biological & Agricultural
Engineering)** — national leader in green stormwater infrastructure (bioretention, permeable
pavement, regenerative conveyance) who *partner with NC DEQ to codify designs into state guidance* →
direct relevance + a route to design-standard adoption.

## 1. UNC Innovate / Innovate Carolina ([start-here page](https://innovate.unc.edu/startup-accelerators-and-venture-services/))

**Gated to faculty/research-affiliated (NOT plain alumni):**
- **KickStart Commercialization Grant-Awards** — **$5K–$50K** non-dilutive for technical/business
  milestones. **Eligibility: a UNC faculty founder + startup based on UNC IP / a UNC research innovation.**
  ✅ **Verified [C-018]:** amount + faculty-founder + UNC-IP gate confirmed verbatim; **no non-faculty-alum
  direct path** exists (Arik-as-alum needs a UNC faculty partner). (The ">$600M raised" figure is unverified.)
  ([KickStart](https://innovate.unc.edu/startup-accelerators-and-venture-services/kickstart-venture-services/kickstart-venture-services-fund/kickstart-grant-awards/))
- **OTC Technology Development (Translational) Grants** — up to **$50K** (typical $5K–$25K), tied to UNC
  inventors/IP. ✅ **Verified [C-019]:** requires a **current full/part-time UNC employee** developing a
  University-created invention — an unaffiliated alum is not eligible.
  ([OTC](https://innovate.unc.edu/office-of-technology-commercialization/inventors/translational-research-funding/))
- **Venture Catalyst Program** — pairs UNC-affiliated startups with grad/postdoc fellows (free
  business-dev support). ([Venture Catalyst](https://innovate.unc.edu/venture-catalyst-program/))

**What an alum CAN tap directly:**
- **Alumni startup support** — mentors, business coaches, essential services (legal, accounting). The
  clearest alum-eligible offering: advisory, not cash.
  ([Start a Venture](https://innovate.unc.edu/resources/start-a-venture/))
- **Adams Apprenticeship** network (application cohort is students, but a useful alumni network).

**Action:** use alum mentor/legal/accounting support now; collaborate with a UNC faculty inventor
(Gillings ESE / Institute for the Environment) to reach KickStart/OTC money. Confirm alum eligibility
nuances with KickStart staff directly.

## 2. NC IDEA — statewide non-dilutive grants (OPEN, no university tie)

Most important **directly-accessible** non-dilutive source. Two cycles/yr (Spring ~late Jan, Fall
~late July); you may apply to only one of MICRO/SEED per cycle. ([NC IDEA](https://ncidea.org/))

| Program | Amount | Fit / Eligibility | Cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SEED** ✅[C-020] | **$50K** non-dilutive | High-growth/scalable. NC HQ + ≥1 **full-time** founder in NC. Marquee target. | ~~Jan 26–Feb 23, 2026~~ → **Fall 2026: opens Jul 27, deadline Aug 24, 2026 (5pm EST)** |
| **MICRO** ✅[C-021] | **$10K** non-dilutive | Earlier-stage milestone validation. NC HQ + ≥1 founder in NC (need not be full-time). Only one of MICRO/SEED per cycle. | **Deadline Aug 24, 2026** (Fall) |
| **LABS** (fka Groundwork) | Free, no equity | Mentorship for startups too early for a grant. | Rolling |
| ENGAGE / ECOSYSTEM | ≤$5K / ≤$100K | Fund *events/organizations*, not the company. | Periodic / Annual |

Sources: [SEED](https://ncidea.org/nc-idea-seed/) · [SEED eligibility](https://ncidea.org/seed-grant-eligibility/) ·
[MICRO](https://ncidea.org/nc-idea-micro/) · [Spring 2026](https://ncidea.org/spring-2026-grant-cycle/).
Also note **NC Tweener Fund** (equity, w/ Triangle Tweener Fund) as a downstream capital relationship.

**Recommendation:** SEED is the marquee target; if too early this cycle, do MICRO + LABS, then SEED
next cycle.

## 3. Local accelerators / incubators (Chapel Hill & Triangle)

- **Launch Chapel Hill** — industry-agnostic, **open to founders with no UNC tie**; wants some
  customer discovery; **encourages solo founders to add a team member first**; fee $1,500/team
  (subsidies available). ([Accelerator](https://www.launchchapelhill.com/accelerator))
  - **Launch, Powered by KPMG** — competitive, MVP-stage "ready to pilot with enterprise customers."
    **Cohort 3: apply Jan 5–29, 2026; program Mar 9–Apr 14, 2026.** Good once a deployable pilot
    exists. ([KPMG accelerator](https://www.launchchapelhill.com/kpmg-accelerator))
- **American Underground (Durham)** — major hub (~250 companies); Google for Startups tech hub;
  returning to American Tobacco Campus ~late Feb 2026. Open via membership.
  ([American Underground](https://americanunderground.com/))
- **CED / Venture Connect** — region's main entrepreneur org. **Venture Connect 2026** has an explicit
  **next-gen hardware & deep-tech spotlight** — on-theme. 2026 pitch apps closed; attend/network,
  target 2027 pitch. ([CED](https://cednc.org/venture-connect/))

## 4. NC State & Duke (for outside founders)

- **NC State Andrews Launch Accelerator** — mainly NC State founders; partner-network touchpoint
  otherwise. ([Andrews](https://entrepreneurship.ncsu.edu/andrews-launch-accelerator/))
- **Duke I&E** — mostly Duke-student/alumni; for outsiders the value is the Experts network/events +
  American Underground proximity. Not a direct funding source for non-Duke founders.
  ([Duke I&E](https://entrepreneurship.duke.edu/about/ecosystem/))

## 5. NC climate/water/cleantech programs, angels & state SBIR/STTR money

**Cleantech accelerators:**
- **Joules Accelerator — Cleantech Catalyst** (Charlotte, statewide) — **90-day, free, no equity**;
  connects cleantech startups to NC utilities/industry/municipalities for a **joint pilot/demo.**
  Strongly relevant — drainage could pilot with NC municipalities.
  ([Joules](https://www.joulesaccelerator.com/), [NC Cleantech Corridor](https://www.nccleantechcorridor.org/accelerator))
- **Research Triangle Cleantech Cluster (RTCC)** — partnership/pilot channel (Duke Energy, ABB, etc.).

**Angels / early-stage VC (deeptech/hardware-friendly):**
- **IDEA Fund Partners** (Durham) — one of the SE's most active **seed funds**; IT, **materials,
  hardware/medical-device**, strong **university-spinout** ties → natural early-equity target.
  ([IDEA Fund](https://directory.ncbiotech.org/company/idea-fund-partners))
- **RTP Capital Associates** (angel network), **Triangle Angel Partners**, **RTP Angel Fund** — the
  RTP-region pre-seed/seed angel cluster. ([RTP Capital](https://www.rtpcapital.org/about-us/our-mission/))

**State non-dilutive — the SBIR/STTR multiplier:**
- **One NC Small Business Program** (NC Commerce) — two parts: **Incentive Funds** reimburse the cost
  of preparing a federal Phase I SBIR/STTR app (FY2026 pool **$200K**); **Matching Funds** = **50% of a
  secured federal Phase I award, ❌ up to ~~$60K~~ → $75K [C-022]** (the $60K was FY2024; FY2026 cap is
  $75,000). FY24-25: 90 businesses, $4.12M. **Cleanest way to stack a federal STTR + 50% state match.**
  ⚠️ FY2026 window closes **2026-06-30** ("or until funds exhausted") — target the next fiscal cycle.
  ([One NC](https://www.commerce.nc.gov/grants-incentives/technology-funds/one-north-carolina-small-business-program))
- **NCInnovation** — **$500M state-endowment** program funding **NC public-university applied research**
  through the valley of death (TRL 3–6). **Private companies NOT eligible** → reachable only *through* a
  university research partner. ✅ **Verified [C-016]:** $500M + TRL 3–6 + private-company exclusion confirmed
  verbatim (THE keystone holds). ⚠️ **[C-017]** the per-grant range "$300K–$1.1M" is **unconfirmed** (only on a
  WAF-blocked policy PDF). ([NCInnovation](https://ncinnovation.org/grants-overview/))

  portfolio, incl. 2026 data-center water-policy work); a channel to **state/local pilot deployments**
  + validation. ([NC Collaboratory](https://collaboratory.unc.edu/))
- **Duke Nicholas School / Nicholas Institute** — secondary environment/sustainability partner.
