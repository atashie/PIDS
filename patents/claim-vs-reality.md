# Granted Claim vs. As-Built Reality

*A short integrity note (per Guardrail #1). Not a legal analysis; for precise scope, consult counsel and the file wrapper in [`PIDS/`](PIDS/). To be expanded later.*

## The point
As prosecuted, **claim 1 of U.S. App. 18/632,186 requires** the conveyance channels to "comprise a top layer and a **water-impermeable bottom layer**." But the specification (and the system as typically deployed) treats that impermeable (bentonite) layer as **optional and locational** — used only where downward percolation is undesirable (e.g., near foundations, roads, sidewalks) — and even describes an embodiment where a channel is filled **entirely with coarse sand**.

## Why it matters to us
- **Honesty in how we describe PIDS.** When we explain "how PIDS works," we should not imply every channel has an impermeable bottom. In the off-site-discharge-eliminating mode, channels are meant to pass water *downward* to the infiltration tunnels.
- **Scope awareness.** What is *claimed* (and thus protected) may be narrower than the full invention as described — relevant to the forward IP strategy (continuations) and to any marketing claims.
